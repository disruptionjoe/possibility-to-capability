# Charter: Possibility to Capability

Status: ratified by Joe on 2026-07-14; agent-capability role clarified and ratified by Joe on 2026-07-16.

## Repository Name

**Possibility to Capability**

Repository slug: `possibility-to-capability`

Visibility: public.

## North Star

Develop the smallest rigorous, repo-independent account of how admissible
possibilities become dynamics, records, access, capability, and finality, and
use it to determine what kind of change actually occurred across heterogeneous
domains without manufacturing agreement.

## Agent-Capability Role

This repository tests whether research agents can perform **typed change
reasoning**: distinguish something becoming possible, dynamically selected,
recorded, accessible, resourced, executable, or final, rather than collapsing
those changes into one vague story of progress or novelty.

The agent must be able to take heterogeneous evidence, preserve each source's
meaning and authority, declare the comparison frame and resource budget, give
ordinary completion and redescription rivals their strongest hearing, and
return either a typed classification or an honest abstention. Apparent
opposites may both survive when they belong to different claim classes, such
as global containment and operational capability.

Physics supplies severe specimens because its tasks, resources, invariances,
and counterfactuals can often be stated sharply. Physics is not the scope of
the capability under test. The same discipline must transfer without analogy
collapse to computation, security, organizations, institutions, biology, and
other domains with source-grounded tasks and interventions.

This is a research and evaluation role, not evidence that agents already have
the capability. Reproducible disagreement, forced abstention, or failure to
transfer across domains is a first-class result.

## Guiding Hypothesis

**Can a representation-respecting hierarchy from possibility through dynamics,
records, access, capability, and finality reliably distinguish fixed-family
disclosure from genuine capability enlargement, remain invariant under
admissible reformulation, and survive explicit null-class and completion tests
when applied to frozen evidence packets from real physical and nonphysical
cases?**

This is a falsifiable research question, not a presumed conclusion.

The hypothesis fails or requires substantial revision if:

- its levels cannot be defined independently of GU-, TI-, or TaF-specific
  assumptions;
- the distinctions collapse under admissible changes of representation;
- every apparent capability change factors through an agreed fixed-family
  completion or null class;
- source repositories cannot be connected without silently changing their
  claims, scopes, or proof standards; or
- no meaningful frozen case survives the full gate sequence, or the same
  definitions cannot transfer beyond one domain without ad hoc reinterpretation.

## Founding Motivation

This repository is founded in response to a concrete joint-seam result first
encountered in the GU Formalization program on 2026-07-14.

GU's source-action work forced the relative coefficients of the relevant
construction through Schur equivariance, reducing the remaining physical
ambiguity to a single binary datum: the grading sign of the interacting
C-operator, equivalently the spectral-section choice governing the record-count
mode and the bar-(b) loop-unitarity question. A separate analysis established
that this bit is decoupled from GU's unresolved spacetime-signature choice.

Five independent methods then attacked whether GU's own good-stable structure
could determine that remaining bit:

1. counterfactual invariance and metric-from-stabilizer;
2. Dirac-BRST cohomology;
3. Lawvere fixed-point consistency;
4. topos internal logic and section classification; and
5. Helmholtz inverse-variational analysis.

As represented by the source result to be imported, all five methods converged
on the same negative conclusion: GU's present structure can locate and
characterize the binary grading datum but cannot select between its admissible
branches. The sign is independent of GU's current internal forcing structure
and can be settled only by an additional datum at the GU / Temporal Issuance /
Time as Finality boundary.

This is the first worked instance of the joint-seam object this repository
exists to study:

- a source theory forces almost the entire construction;
- independent methods locate one residual datum;
- the residual cannot be decided inside the source theory;
- the unresolved choice changes physical capability or admissibility; and
- its resolution belongs to a typed boundary among repositories rather than
  to any one source repository.

The result remains GU-owned source truth. This repository owns only the frozen
representation of that result, the neutral gate analysis performed upon it,
and any repo-independent conclusions established from that analysis.

The founding case does not make this a GU repository. GU supplies the first
specimen because it exposed the ownership gap with unusual precision.

## What This Repository Owns

This repository owns the repo-independent research layer, including:

- the possibility -> dynamics -> records -> access -> capability -> finality
  hierarchy;
- precise definitions of its levels, transitions, equivalences, and failure
  modes;
- the abstract GU/TI/TaF joint-seam object, without absorbing the source
  theories;
- null classes, completion tests, construction forks, and cross-layer gates;
- packet schemas and interface contracts for importing frozen results;
- synthesis results proved from correctly imported packets;
- negative results, incompatibility results, and failed syntheses;
- the distinction between a located obstruction and a forced resolution;
- typed change adjudication that distinguishes description, access, resources,
  operational capability, containment, and finality across domains; and
- its own hypotheses, experiments, literature map, and explanatory models.

A theorem proved entirely within this repository may become this repository's
truth. A statement imported from another repository remains that source
repository's truth.

## What This Repository Coordinates but Does Not Own

This repository may coordinate:

- requests for frozen evidence or verdict packets from GU, Temporal Issuance,
  Time as Finality, WI-069, and other research repositories;
- cross-repository comparison and interface testing;
- questions whose resolution requires more than one source repository;
- handoffs through repository mailboxes and explicit cross-repository gates;
  and
- attention-relevant adjudication signals supplied to JoeOps for Joe's
  prioritization.

It does not own:

- GU's definitions, proofs, claim status, or canonical truth;
- Temporal Issuance's definitions, proofs, claim status, or canonical truth;
- Time as Finality's definitions, proofs, claim status, or canonical truth;
- WI-069's emergence, obstruction, or transduction research;
- JoeOps attention authority or attention-dependent orchestration;
- source-repository edits; or
- authority to upgrade a source claim from plausible to proved.

Source repositories own their source truth. This repository owns only the
neutral joint layer and conclusions independently established here. Cross-repo
work is orchestrated by the owner or workflow designated by its governing
protocol. JoeOps enters when Joe's attention, prioritization, or decision is
required.

The boundary is scientific adjudication versus operational orchestration. P2C
may decide what a valid combination of frozen packets establishes and may
coordinate its authorized packet and interface workflows. It does not thereby
own every cross-repo workflow. JoeOps owns Joe's attention and the cross-repo
orchestration that specifically depends on that attention, not cross-repo
orchestration in general.

## Relationship to WI-069

This repository and WI-069 are distinct sibling research objects. Neither
subsumes the other.

WI-069 studies class-relative obstruction, measurement transitions, incentive
or value transduction, collapse prevention, and open-ended emergence across
heterogeneous domains.

Possibility to Capability studies the more foundational classification
problem: what type of transition occurred, at which explanatory level it is
located, whether it is forced rather than merely represented, and what survives
explicit completion and null-class challenges.

Their relationship is a typed, bidirectional research interface:

- Possibility to Capability may offer WI-069 a hierarchy, null classes, and
  gates for distinguishing disclosure from capability enlargement.
- WI-069 may offer Possibility to Capability difficult cross-domain cases that
  stress-test whether the hierarchy is genuinely general.
- Neither repository imports the other's conclusions automatically.
- Exchange occurs only through frozen, provenance-bearing packets with
  explicit scope and claim status.

## First Deliverable

The first real deliverable is a frozen-packet schema developed and stress-tested
against the founding GU case, rather than a hierarchy developed only in the
abstract.

### Deliverable 1A: Frozen-Packet Schema v0.1

The schema must preserve at least:

- source repository and immutable source revision;
- source-issued packet identifier;
- exact question decided;
- definitions and notation;
- construction choice and retained construction forks;
- assumptions and quantifiers;
- method-by-method result ledger;
- proof, computation, argument, and exploration grades;
- verification and replication status;
- claim status in the source repository;
- scope of any independence or impossibility result;
- admissible alternatives or countermodels;
- named residuals and known failure conditions;
- statements explicitly not established;
- source-repository ownership;
- requested boundary interfaces; and
- a content hash or equivalent freezing mechanism.

### Deliverable 1B: Founding Packet #1

Packet #1 imports the GU C-operator grading-sign result as a source-issued
frozen packet. It separately preserves:

- the Schur-forced source-action coefficients;
- the signature-decoupling result;
- the precise binary grading datum;
- the five independent methods and the scope of each;
- the exact meaning of independence from GU's current forcing structure;
- the distinction between locating the datum and selecting its value;
- the unchanged status of bar (b);
- the TI and TaF boundary dependencies; and
- every caveat, construction fork, and verification grade carried by GU.

The packet is not complete merely because five method names are present. Each
leg must be traceable to a completed source artifact with its own grade and
provenance. If GU has not frozen all five method results into an importable
source packet, the correct result is `NOT_YET_IMPORTABLE`, with the missing
source gates named.

### Deliverable 1C: First Gate Run

Packet #1 is then run through:

1. **Provenance gate** - every statement traces to frozen source truth.
2. **Construction gate** - material construction choices and forks are explicit.
3. **Formation gate** - one coherent joint object forms without changing source
   meanings.
4. **Completion/null gate** - the residual survives agreed completions and null
   classes.
5. **Capability gate** - the choice changes capability rather than only
   representation, access, or description.
6. **Finality gate** - the selecting datum is genuinely external to the source
   theory and its owner is identified.
7. **No-Artificial-Success gate** - one argument satisfies the whole chain.
8. **Neutrality gate** - the verdict would be unchanged if the physical branch
   labels were reversed.

The verdict may be positive, negative, split, blocked on source provenance, or
evidence that the hierarchy needs revision. No outcome is preferred in advance.

## Protected Boundaries

### Frozen-Packet Rule

Cross-repository evidence is imported only as a frozen packet containing its
source, version or commit, definitions, assumptions, quantifiers, proof status,
confidence, known limitations, and unresolved questions. This repository never
edits the imported packet's substantive content.

### Source-Sovereignty Rule

Every source repository retains authority over its definitions, results, and
claim status. A synthesis may identify a consequence, incompatibility, or
missing premise, but it cannot retroactively modify source truth.

### Located-Is-Not-Forced Rule

Showing where a question is resolved is not the same as proving which
resolution nature selects. Localization, consistency, necessity, uniqueness,
and empirical selection remain separate.

### No-Artificial-Success Rule

Partial successes from different repositories may not be combined into a joint
success unless one explicit argument satisfies all relevant assumptions and
gates simultaneously.

### Construction-Fork Rule

When a result depends on a noncanonical construction choice, all materially
admissible branches remain live until a theorem, invariant, or empirical
discriminator removes them.

### Failure-Preservation Rule

A failed synthesis, empty survivor set, incompatibility proof, or demonstration
that the hierarchy is not universal is a first-class result.

### Publication Rule

External publication, posting, outreach, or representation of a result as a
cross-repository conclusion requires Joe's explicit authorization and the
required source-repository gates.

## Neutrality Rule

This repository roots for no source repository, preferred mechanism, or desired
unification.

Its measure of success is sharper discrimination among:

1. ordinary disclosure within a fixed admissible family;
2. dynamics that change records or accessibility without enlarging capability;
3. genuine enlargement or restriction of operational capability;
4. finality or closure that cannot be reduced to preceding levels; and
5. evidence that the hierarchy itself is incomplete or mistaken.

A null result is successful when it is the result warranted by the evidence.
