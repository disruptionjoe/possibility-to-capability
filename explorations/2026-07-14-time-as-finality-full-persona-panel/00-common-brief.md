# Common Brief: What Is the Real Story of Possibility to Capability?

Every persona receives this same brief and must work independently. Do not read
another persona's response before completing your own.

## The question

From your named disciplinary perspective, steelman the strongest plausible
account of what the new Possibility to Capability repository is really about.

A steelman is the most coherent and consequential interpretation you think the
current evidence can support. It is not an endorsement. State what the story
would mean if right, why it is worth taking seriously, and what would make it
wrong.

## Current candidate story

The current candidate says physical explanations answer distinct typed
questions:

- what states, histories, transformations, or alternatives are admissible;
- how systems transform within an assessed family;
- which distinctions stabilize as records;
- which records or possibilities become observable, reachable, conditionable,
  or controllable;
- whether the normalized operational task set genuinely enlarges or
  contracts, rather than merely becoming visible or accessible; and
- whether any settlement remains a candidate for finality after strong,
  preregistered attempts to factor it through the preceding types and reopen it
  by admissible continuation.

The arrow is a typed partial diagnostic, not a chronology, causal ladder,
total order, ontology, or claim that every case visits every type. Cases may
skip types, instantiate several, split by construction, remain incomparable,
or force `HIERARCHY_REVISION`.

## Live rivals

1. Reductive dynamics/coarse-graining: a complete enough dynamical and boundary
   model explains records, access, capability, and apparent finality.
2. Operational-context primacy: capability is constituted by agents, tasks,
   resources, access, and control, so the proposed normalization is not
   generally invariant.
3. Explanatory plurality: no single cross-domain diagnostic grammar survives
   without erasing source-specific structure.
4. Constraint-first structure: all six diagnoses are projections of one deeper
   consistency or constraint object.
