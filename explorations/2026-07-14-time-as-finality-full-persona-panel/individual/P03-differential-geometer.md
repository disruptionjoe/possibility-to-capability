## Steelman thesis

Possibility to Capability is building an atlas for explanatory structure: source-owned cases provide local charts, while the receiver tests whether possibility, dynamics, records, access, capability, and candidate finality define stable strata of one comparison space rather than a universal sequence. Its deepest ambition is to discover which diagnoses descend through changes of representation and which are genuine construction-dependent forks or obstructions.

## Summary of understanding

The repository separates six questions that are often conflated, then treats their relation as typed, partial, and nonexclusive. Geometrically, admissible states and histories resemble a base or moduli space; dynamics supplies flows or transport within a chosen family; records are persistent structures; access specifies which structures can be probed or controlled; capability compares operational task sets after declared factors are held fixed; and finality is only a bounded obstruction claim after settlement, factorization, and continuation tests. Frozen packets prevent the receiver from changing the source chart while constructing this atlas. Synthetic tests show only that the proposed classifier preserves distinctions, nulls, forks, blocking, and hierarchy revision. No real packet has completed the geometry.

## Strongest insight

The rejection of a ladder in favor of a stratified or singular space is exactly right. Physical classifications often fail to form a smooth global manifold: local descriptions overlap imperfectly, strata meet, singular cases occur, and no canonical ordering exists. Treating `CONSTRUCTION_FORK`, incomparability, and `HIERARCHY_REVISION` as legitimate outcomes makes the instrument capable of detecting failure of its own atlas rather than forcing every case into one coordinate system.

## Strongest criticism

The access/capability separation depends on a normalization frame, and a frame that “holds fixed” representation, resources, access, and control is analogous to choosing a connection—a horizontal/vertical splitting. Such a splitting is generally not canonical. Unless capability descends to an invariant quotient under admissible changes of frame, the classifier may mistake gauge choice for physical structure. The current evidence establishes only label-swap stability, not invariance under substantive changes of chart.

## Hidden assumptions

The program assumes that physically equivalent constructions form a sufficiently coherent groupoid; that task sets can be transported between cases; that a common normalization is not conclusion-entailing; that relevant families admit stable boundaries and continuation rules; that persistent records and access relations survive representation change; and that a finite diagnostic vocabulary can represent singular or bifurcating cases. Finality additionally assumes that the admissible extension class is specified independently of the desired obstruction.

## Rose

Its strongest present feature is disciplined locality. Every verdict is indexed by packet, construction, argument chain, and search scope. That is the right posture for geometric reasoning, where a local statement must not be promoted to a global invariant without transition data and descent.

## Bud

The most promising development is a representation challenge using genuinely equivalent formulations. If independent reviewers can recover the same diagnosis and capability comparison across nontrivial source-certified transformations, the vocabulary may begin to behave like an intrinsic structure rather than a convenient chart.

## Thorn

The most dangerous failure is hidden non-canonicity: reviewers repeatedly choose normalizations, equivalences, and continuation classes that make the preferred strata appear stable. A polished taxonomy could then encode gauge fixing while presenting it as invariant physics.

## Confidence

**8/10** that this atlas-and-descent interpretation best captures what the repository is about. This rates the framing, not the truth, universality, or physical success of the candidate; those remain untested on accepted real evidence.

## Suggested experiment

Take one real source-issued packet that has two source-certified, physically equivalent but formally distinct representations. Freeze the transformation map, two independently proposed normalization frames, the task vocabulary, and predicted invariant fields before classification. Run both branches separately. Compare not just terminal labels but witnesses, task-set relations, factorization paths, nulls, and blocks. A disagreement must remain a fork or hierarchy revision, never be reconciled after seeing the result.

## Suggested theorem or mathematical direction

Model admissible constructions as a groupoid (eventually a differentiable stack if justified) and the diagnostic data as a partial functor or constructible sheaf into a category containing the six types plus null, blocked, fork, incomparable, and revision outcomes. Seek a descent theorem: source-certified equivalences induce isomorphic diagnostic data, while genuinely different constructions remain distinct fibers. For capability, identify conditions under which normalized task sets form a quotient independent of the chosen connection. For finality, formulate only a relative obstruction-to-extension statement indexed by the frozen continuation class.

## Suggested falsification test

Falsify the representation-respecting claim if a source-certified equivalence changes a diagnosis or normalized capability relation after every load-bearing witness is transported. Falsify a finality candidate if it vanishes on an admissible refinement, cover, embedding, or continuation that was not excluded independently in advance. Repeated failures of descent should favor construction-relative plurality or a constraint-first object.

## Relationship classification

**Analogy.** Differential geometry supplies a precise analogy—charts, strata, connections, gauge dependence, descent, and obstruction—but no functor from physical cases to such geometric objects has been constructed, and no theorem identifies the candidate vocabulary with manifold or bundle structure. Calling the relation homology, reduction, or equivalence would outrun the evidence.
