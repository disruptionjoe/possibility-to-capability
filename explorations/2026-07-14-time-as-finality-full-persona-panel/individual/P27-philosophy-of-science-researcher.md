## Steelman thesis

Possibility to Capability is really a methodology for making cross-theory explanatory change criticizable: it asks whether an apparent novelty is a change in representation, dynamics, records, access, normalized operation, or a still-residual settlement, while preventing semantic translation and auxiliary choices from manufacturing the verdict. Its deepest ambition is not a six-level ontology but a disciplined way to tell genuine theoretical gain from redescription, accommodation, and premature closure.

## Summary of understanding

The repository proposes a typed, partial diagnostic rather than a historical ladder. Its packet and gate machinery separates source claims from receiver judgments, requires construction and premise dependencies to remain visible, and treats blocking, nulls, forks, rival explanations, and hierarchy revision as legitimate outcomes. In philosophy-of-science terms, it is trying to operationalize severe intertheoretic testing under theory-ladenness and underdetermination.

The present achievement is methodological, not physical. Synthetic cases show that the instrument can preserve distinctions it was designed to preserve; the GU preflight shows that it can stop when provenance is insufficient. With zero accepted real packets and zero completed physical gate runs, neither the candidate hierarchy nor any rival has received real-case confirmation here.

## Strongest insight

The crucial insight is that "what changed?" must be typed before it is explained. A newly observable option is not automatically a new capability; a persistent trace is not automatically accessible; an unexplained settlement is not automatically finality. This blocks a common pattern in theory change: promoting a shift in description, experimental access, or auxiliary conditions into a claim of new ontology or physical necessity.

## Strongest criticism

The framework may be too methodologically elastic to risk decisive loss. `BLOCKED`, `INDETERMINATE`, `CONSTRUCTION_FORK`, and `HIERARCHY_REVISION` are scientifically responsible outcomes, but together they can form a protective belt around the six-type core. Unless the program specifies which repeated patterns require abandoning--not merely refining--the taxonomy, falsifiability remains procedural rather than substantive. The one-chain rule could also reject legitimate convergent support when science reasonably depends on several partially independent arguments.

## Hidden assumptions

- Source meaning can be frozen and transported without material semantic loss.
- Physically equivalent representations can be certified independently of the receiver's preferred diagnosis.
- A common task vocabulary and normalization frame can be chosen without encoding the capability verdict.
- Factorization and admissible-continuation searches can be bounded without insulating finality from its strongest rivals.
- Cross-domain explanatory types possess enough invariance to justify comparison.
- Better discrimination or compression is available to distinguish the candidate from mere bookkeeping.

## Rose

The provenance stop is the current strongest result. Refusing to convert visible GU artifacts into independent evidential units demonstrates unusually good control of evidential lineage, auxiliary dependence, and claim ownership.

## Bud

The ranked tests could turn the repository into a genuine comparative methodology: candidate and rivals make preregistered, construction-specific forecasts, then update symmetrically on real cases. A public record of which framework predicted each blocked, forked, revised, or stable diagnosis would be more informative than another taxonomy refinement.

## Thorn

The most dangerous failure is an elegant research bureaucracy that continually improves its gates while never admitting evidence capable of changing the core picture. Continued intake failure must eventually count against the feasibility of the cross-repository object, not only in favor of procedural rigor.

## Confidence

8/10 that this is the best interpretation of what the repository is about. The artifacts consistently privilege error control, rival discrimination, and revisability over any positive claim about finality or the six types' universality.

## Suggested experiment

Run one source-issued real packet in two source-certified equivalent representations. Before inspection, have independent teams preregister the candidate diagnosis, the strongest reductive diagnosis, the operational-context construction, the factorization scope, and distinct intervention or prediction consequences. Blind receiver evaluators to branch labels. The test should score semantic preservation, diagnostic stability, and discriminative payoff--not merely gate completion.

## Suggested theorem or mathematical direction

Define diagnosis as a partial map from frozen packet, construction, normalization frame, and factorization scope to outcomes. Prove an invariance theorem under an explicit category of source-certified equivalences, or prove a no-go theorem showing that access and capability cannot be separated invariantly without additional structure. Either result would sharpen the program: the first licenses comparison; the second localizes unavoidable context dependence.

## Suggested falsification test

Preregister a repair budget across several heterogeneous real packets. Falsify the current six-type core if source-certified equivalent representations repeatedly change diagnoses, if stable missing types recur, or if a reductive or constraint-first rival matches every successful discrimination with fewer independent choices. Do not reset the count by renaming each failure a new construction.

## Relationship classification

**Homology.** Philosophy of science and this candidate share the same underlying structure: theories are tested only together with representation choices, auxiliaries, measurement access, and translation rules. The repository is a domain-specific realization of familiar problems of underdetermination, severe testing, and theory change--not merely a verbal analogy, but not a reduction or equivalence either.
