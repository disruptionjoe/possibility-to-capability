# P48 — Gauge Theory Researcher

## Steelman thesis

The repository is building a gauge-conscious diagnostic for separating changes of description from changes of physical orbit structure, observable records, operational reach, and genuinely normalized capability. Its finality question is whether any residual selection survives quotienting, constraint reduction, boundary completion, and admissible continuation without being a gauge-fixing artifact.

## Summary of understanding

The six diagnoses are not a temporal cascade. They are typed questions applied to a frozen case through provenance, construction, formation, completion/null, capability, finality, artificial-success, and neutrality gates. Synthetic controls show that the machinery can distinguish relabeling, dynamics, records, access, task-set change, and bounded finality candidates while retaining forks and revision. That result concerns the classifier under stipulated witnesses. Zero real packets are accepted. GU-001 is provenance-blocked, so its visible source material cannot support a receiver-side gauge, capability, or finality conclusion.

In gauge theory, possibility begins with configuration space, constraints, bundles, boundary sectors, and the quotient by gauge transformations. Dynamics concerns the equations and allowed evolution. Records require gauge-invariant or relationally stable distinctions. Access concerns which observables can be measured or controlled. Capability should compare task sets on reduced physical state space under a declared common resource frame. Candidate finality would have to survive gauge reduction and every admissible enlargement of boundary or sector data.

## Strongest insight

The repository’s construction-fork and neutrality rules encode good gauge hygiene. A favorable branch cannot be selected merely because a gauge, boundary convention, or labeling makes it visible. The demand that one coherent construction pass the entire chain is especially important when local gauges work separately but do not define a global physical object.

## Strongest criticism

Gauge reduction is not merely relabeling. Orbit spaces can be singular, global gauge fixings can fail, boundary symmetries can become physical charges, and Gribov copies can defeat naive quotienting. The current representation control is therefore too weak. A task-set normalization that “holds representation and access fixed” may erase precisely the edge modes, superselection sectors, or relational observables that define physical capability.

## Hidden assumptions

The program assumes a defensible division between gauge redundancy and physical symmetry; a common observable algebra across constructions; compatible treatment of boundary conditions; and a noncircular resource normalization. It also assumes that receiver formation can respect constraints without importing source-specific gauge choices as universal structure.

## Rose

The strongest current asset is the refusal to convert visibility into physical enlargement. Gauge theory repeatedly teaches that more coordinates, more components, or a more convenient gauge does not mean more degrees of freedom or capability.

## Bud

A gauge-aware packet profile could make the framework substantially sharper by requiring the gauge group, constraint surface, reduced space or observable algebra, boundary symmetries, anomaly status, global sectors, and maps between admissible gauge fixings.

## Thorn

The most dangerous failure is quotienting away real boundary or relational structure and then declaring the surviving normalization invariant. The opposite failure—counting gauge copies as capability—would be equally fatal.

## Confidence

8/10 that this is the strongest gauge-theoretic reading of the project. It is supported as a conceptual steelman and by synthetic anti-collapse controls, not by real gauge-theory evidence. No physical gate result or GU conclusion is available here.

## Suggested theorem or mathematical direction

Prove a reduction-commutation theorem: under stated regularity and anomaly-free conditions, diagnostic classification before and after symplectic or BRST reduction yields the same physical result. Then characterize failures—singular strata, anomalies, boundary charges, or nontrivial orbit topology—as explicit construction forks or hierarchy-revision witnesses rather than noise.

## Suggested falsification test

Reject the current diagnostic if legitimate gauge-equivalent formulations give different capability or finality outcomes, or if removing a gauge choice changes the task-set comparison with no physical observable change. Conversely, if all purported capability enlargement disappears on reduced phase space, classify it as representation or access rather than preserve the preferred verdict.
