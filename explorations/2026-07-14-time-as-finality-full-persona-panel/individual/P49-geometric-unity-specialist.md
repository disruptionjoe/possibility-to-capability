## Steelman thesis

The repository is a neutral seam tribunal for a question that a source theory cannot answer merely by displaying where a residual choice lives: what exactly has been forced, what remains admissible, whether the residual changes normalized physical capability, and which boundary could lawfully supply a selector. Its strongest purpose is to keep “located” from being promoted to “selected” while preserving the exact source construction.

## Summary of understanding

Possibility to Capability owns neither GU definitions nor GU verdicts. It owns packet contracts, receiver gates, a typed diagnostic vocabulary, and repo-local synthesis conclusions. The present machinery has passed deterministic synthetic tests and has correctly stopped one real intake. At the pinned GU revision, no source-issued GU-001 packet, canonical manifest, or digest bundle existed. The receiver therefore records `NOT_YET_IMPORTABLE`; provenance ran and blocked, while construction through neutrality did not run.

That boundary is central to the steelman. A GU specialist may recognize source-local artifacts, status strings, and a motivating residual datum, but the receiver cannot reconstruct a packet from them, count visible methods as independent evidence, or infer capability and finality. The real story is not that this repository has completed the GU/TI/TaF seam. It is that it has specified what a legitimate seam result would have to preserve and what it must not manufacture.

## Strongest insight

The distinction between source forcing and receiver classification is the sharpest insight. A nearly forced construction can still contain a discrete residual. Locating that residual, proving its independence under stated premises, showing that branches differ physically, and identifying a lawful selector are four different achievements. The eight-gate chain makes those distinctions auditable.

## Strongest criticism

The central motivation remains scientifically absent from the receiver because the packet does not exist. That means the repository risks building a generic architecture around an imagined idealization of GU rather than the exact source object. Packetization may also flatten load-bearing details—representation choices, shared premises, boundary conditions, source-local grades—unless GU itself issues the dependency structure and joint argument.

## Hidden assumptions

The program assumes that GU can express the relevant result in a frozen packet without loss; that source and receiver constructions can be mapped without changing meaning; that signature, grading, and boundary choices remain separately typed; that branch labels can be reversed without changing hidden physical content; and that capability consequences can be defined independently of the receiver’s preferred interpretation.

Formalize a source-to-receiver preservation theorem. Given an immutable source object with an automorphism group, premise-dependency graph, and residual branch set, specify conditions under which the receiver preserves forced structure, independence wording, and branch distinctions. Then prove a separate criterion for when branch variation induces a normalized capability difference. No theorem should infer a selector from localization alone.

It also fails if a simpler source-local account captures every distinction without the receiver hierarchy.
