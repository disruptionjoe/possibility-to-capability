## Steelman thesis

The repository is constructing a model-risk firewall against mistaking newly observed exposure, improved market access, or an expanded strategy menu for a genuine change in the system’s feasible risk-bearing capacity. Its central promise is a typed stress-testing framework that preserves tail failures and refuses to call residual uncertainty “finality.”

## Summary of understanding

Possibility maps to admissible market states, portfolios, scenarios, and constraints. Dynamics maps to price evolution, trading, margin feedback, and risk transmission. Records are trades, prices, defaults, collateral calls, and accounting marks that persist. Access concerns information, liquidity, venues, counterparties, and instruments that make states or actions reachable. Capability is the normalized set of tasks—hedges, transfers, financings, or loss absorptions—available after holding representation, resources, access, and control fixed. Candidate finality would be a settlement or loss allocation that cannot be explained by those preceding channels and cannot be reopened within a declared continuation class. The partial diagnostic is attractive because crises routinely collapse distinctions among visibility, liquidity, leverage, and solvency.

## Strongest insight

The insistence on explicit nulls, construction forks, and one-chain evidence is unusually appropriate for risk. Diversified-looking methods often share latent premises, data, or calibration regimes; counting them as independent understates correlated model failure. A premise-dependency ledger is more informative than a method count.

## Strongest criticism

The proposed normalization is least credible where it matters most: tails. Resources, access, control, and task definitions are endogenous to stress. Liquidity disappears, correlations jump, collateral rules change, and agents adapt. Holding those factors fixed may describe an artificial local world rather than the relevant capability. Synthetic cases with stipulated witnesses cannot reveal reflexivity, common-mode failure, or model uncertainty in the continuation and factorization classes.

## Hidden assumptions

- A common task and resource frame remains meaningful across stressed regimes.
- Tail dependence is represented rather than hidden by marginal comparisons.
- Source packets preserve selection effects, missing data, and calibration uncertainty.
- The admissible scenario family is not truncated to exclude the decisive loss path.
- Settlement and irreversibility are not confused with legal or operational closeout.
- Independent reviewers can choose normalization and factorization scopes before observing outcomes.

## Rose

The evidence ceiling is well calibrated. Zero accepted real packets and a blocked first intake are stated as such, which prevents operational readiness from being presented as validated risk discrimination.

## Bud

Use the framework on a paired liquidity-versus-solvency episode. One intervention should change market access while balance-sheet loss-bearing capacity is unchanged; another should change the feasible loss-absorption set under the same access conditions.

## Thorn

Finality may become the modeler’s residual bucket for whatever lies beyond the chosen stress horizon. A bounded “no factor found” statement can be cosmetically upgraded into an ontological conclusion if scope is forgotten.

## Confidence

8/10. The risk lens strongly supports the repo as an anti-conflation and model-governance instrument, while making no claim that its physical taxonomy is validated.

## Suggested experiment

Freeze a real, fully provenance-bearing market episode and preregister competing liquidity, solvency, information, and constraint constructions. Classify an access shock and a capital-capacity shock using blind task definitions, then replay them under stressed correlations and endogenous margin rules. Preserve disagreements as construction forks.

## Suggested theorem or mathematical direction

Define capability as a robust feasible-task correspondence over a family of probability measures and stress regimes. Seek invariance under equivalent numeraires and representations, monotonicity under genuine resource enlargement, and explicit failure conditions when tail dependence prevents a stable quotient over access.

## Suggested falsification test

Revise the hierarchy if capability diagnoses reverse under equally admissible stress measures or if every apparent finality is represented as an absorbing state, ruin event, legal settlement, or horizon artifact within a defensible dynamics-and-constraint model.

## Relationship classification

`analogy`. Risk systems provide powerful adversarial examples for the candidate’s distinctions, especially normalization and correlated evidence, but financial settlement and tail loss are engineered and institutional phenomena rather than structural evidence of physical finality.
