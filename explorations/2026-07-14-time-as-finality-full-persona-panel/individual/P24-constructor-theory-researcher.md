## Steelman thesis

Possibility to Capability is an audit framework for modal claims: it asks when a physical account has merely redescribed, reached, or controlled an already-possible task and when the counterfactual repertoire of tasks has genuinely changed. Its deepest ambition is to prevent claims of capability or finality from outrunning the constructors, resources, equivalences, and impossibility statements that would make those claims physical.

## Summary of understanding

In constructor-theoretic language, a task specifies input-output attribute pairs, and a law distinguishes tasks possible to arbitrary accuracy and repeatability from impossible ones. The repository's typed diagnostic can be read as separating: the admissible task domain; transformations within it; information-bearing records; availability of measurement or control; the normalized set of operational tasks; and, only provisionally, settlements resistant to earlier-type factorization and admissible continuation.

This is not yet a constructor theory or a physical hierarchy. The evidence supports a synthetic classifier and disciplined interfaces only: 35 stipulated cases were mechanically discriminated, no real packet has been accepted, no real eight-gate run has completed, and GU-001 is blocked at provenance. Constructor theory supplies an interpretive lens, not additional evidence for the candidate.

## Strongest insight

The repository is really policing counterfactual equivocation. “We can now do X” may mean X was always physically possible but lacked a record, controller, constructor, or known procedure; alternatively, the admissible task algebra may have changed. Requiring explicit normalization and construction branches is exactly the right way to stop access, know-how, reachability, and law-level possibility from being counted as one achievement.

## Strongest criticism

“Possibility” and “capability” risk collapsing. In constructor theory, physical possibility is already a statement about whether a constructor for a task can exist. If descriptions, representations, resources, access, and control are genuinely held fixed, a changed capability set seems to require either a changed task definition, changed substrate/law, or changed admissible ancillary resources—hence a change already located in possibility or dynamics. The repository must show a non-circular residual meaning of capability, not stipulate one through normalization.

## Hidden assumptions

The framework assumes that task identity survives representation change; substrates and attributes are stable enough for before/after comparison; possible/impossible quantifiers include explicit accuracy, repeatability, resource, and ancillary-system conditions; records are physically distinguishable information variables; access can sometimes be factored from task possibility; a common normalization does not encode the verdict; and bounded factorization plus continuation tests are informative without masquerading as absolute impossibility proofs.

## Rose

Its strongest present result is epistemic hygiene. A constructor-theoretic impossibility claim is extremely sensitive to hidden resources and quantifiers; the frozen-packet, construction-fork, one-chain, and failure-preservation rules are unusually well matched to that danger. Stopping GU-001 at provenance demonstrates the discipline rather than the physical story.

## Bud

Recast the six diagnoses as relations in a task-and-constructor algebra. Records become information-media tasks, access becomes availability of discriminators/controllers, capability becomes a resource-indexed ideal of possible tasks, and candidate finality becomes a demanding closure claim under composition, ancillary extension, and admissible constructor construction.

## Thorn

The most dangerous failure is using one word—capability—for nomological possibility, resource-relative feasibility, current reachability, and acquired know-how. If the normalization frame moves these distinctions by choice, the classifier will manufacture its key result while appearing neutral. Finality could then become the same error at a grander scale: “no constructor found” promoted to “no constructor is possible.”

## Confidence

8/10 that this is the strongest constructor-theoretic interpretation of what the repository is about. The confidence concerns explanatory framing, not the truth, novelty, or universality of the physics.

## Suggested experiment

Run the preregistered access-versus-capability test on one controllable physical platform. Pair (A) an intervention that adds a measurement/control interface while preserving the certified reachable-task algebra with (B) a parameter change across a controllability threshold that strictly changes that algebra under the same task vocabulary and resource bounds. Freeze the source's task definitions, error tolerances, Lie-reachability or equivalent witnesses, alternative resource frames, and representation maps before receiver classification. Branch disagreement is a result, not noise.

## Suggested theorem or mathematical direction

Define a symmetric monoidal preorder of tasks, quotiented by source-certified representation equivalences and indexed by declared resource/access/control frames. Seek a separation theorem: access-only interventions preserve the normalized capability ideal, whereas capability change is strict inclusion or incomparability invariant under the quotient. Then characterize when any candidate finality is merely closure or impossibility in this task algebra, and state explicit conditions under which a genuinely additional type would be required.

## Suggested falsification test

Take a real accepted packet with two physically equivalent representations and all materially admissible ancillary-resource branches. If the access/capability verdict changes under equivalence, if no invariant task quotient exists, or if every purported capability change reduces to altered possibility, dynamics, or resources, revise or remove the capability type. If every finality candidate reduces to a scoped impossible-task statement or reopens under composition, remove finality as an independent diagnosis.

## Relationship classification

**Homology.** Both constructor theory and the candidate organize explanation around counterfactual transformations and distinguish what laws permit from what present arrangements expose or execute. The structural correspondence is deeper than analogy, but neither reduction nor equivalence is established: the repository adds provenance, source-sovereignty, record/access diagnostics, and a candidate finality gate that constructor theory does not automatically supply.
