# P31 — AI Learning Theory Researcher

## Steelman thesis

The repository is really about separating information that lies somewhere inside a learning system from information the system can access, use, and generalize with under controlled conditions. It proposes a failure-tolerant type system for deciding when training reveals structure already implicit in a hypothesis family and when it changes the learnable or operational task repertoire.

## Summary of understanding

Possibility maps to hypothesis classes, data-generating families, or admissible representations. Dynamics covers optimization and predictive updating within a specified learner. Records are parameter states, sufficient statistics, replay buffers, or persistent learned features. Access is availability through a decoder, prompt, retrieval mechanism, attention pathway, or interface. Capability is the normalized family of tasks achieved under fixed data, compute, representation, access, and control budgets. Candidate finality would be a settlement that resists a declared family of model extensions, further data, retraining, and alternative factorizations.

The repository has shown only that this machinery behaves coherently on deterministic synthetic fixtures. It has accepted no real frozen packet and completed no real physical gate run. The blocked GU intake does not establish a learning-theoretic or physical conclusion.

## Strongest insight

The distinction between decodability and capability is exactly right. A probe may extract a feature without the trained policy being able to exploit it; a prompt or retrieval index may expose a latent competence; fine-tuning may change sample-efficient generalization; architectural expansion may enlarge the realizable function class. Treating all four as “the model knows it” destroys the explanatory question. A named normalization frame forces the comparison to say what was held fixed.

## Strongest criticism

There may be no task-independent capability object. Generalization is relative to distributions, loss, sample size, computational budget, optimization algorithm, interface, and evaluator. Holding access fixed can remove the mechanism that constitutes competence, while changing the task distribution can reverse every set inclusion. The operational-context rival is therefore especially strong in learning systems.

## Hidden assumptions

The candidate assumes a shared task vocabulary, meaningful equivalence across representations, stable resource accounting, and a non-circular division between representation and readout. It also assumes that “admissible continuation” can be bounded without arbitrarily excluding larger models, new data, tool use, or changed training algorithms that would reopen the verdict.

## Rose

The source/receiver firewall and explicit method-premise structure are unusually well suited to machine-learning claims, where benchmark artifacts, probe results, and narrative interpretations are often silently aggregated. A blocked result remains informative without becoming negative evidence about the source model.

## Bud

The most promising direction is a capability quotient that is invariant to invertible reparameterization and separates latent representational sufficiency, efficient accessibility, and distributionally robust task success. This could sharpen debates about emergence and learned representations without requiring a universal ontology.

## Thorn

Normalization can do all the argumentative work. If the evaluator chooses the task family, compute budget, decoder class, and interface after seeing the result, almost any change can be labeled access-only or capability-enlarging. Formal precision would then conceal benchmark design choices.

## Confidence

8/10 that this steelman captures the repository’s most consequential learning-theoretic purpose. It is not a probability that the hierarchy is universal, that model capabilities are intrinsic, or that finality exists.

## Suggested experiment

Construct a blinded paired study over several learner families. In one arm, keep weights and representation fixed while providing a stronger decoder, retrieval channel, or prompt; in another, keep evaluation access fixed while an intervention changes the learned representation and preregistered held-out task performance. Freeze datasets, seeds, compute, decoder classes, task semantics, and source results before receiver classification. Repeat under alternative but defensible task distributions and resource budgets; retain divergent branches as construction forks.

## Suggested theorem or mathematical direction

Define capability as equivalence classes of task-risk vectors achievable within fixed sample and compute budgets. Prove invariance under invertible representation maps and monotonicity under explicitly ordered access channels, perhaps using Blackwell comparison or statistical experiment theory. Then derive conditions distinguishing improved readout from strict enlargement of the efficiently learnable task class, including lower-bound counterexamples where information is present but computationally inaccessible.

## Suggested falsification test

Reject the proposed boundary if no nontrivial capability quotient survives changes among source-certified equivalent representations, task codings, and admissible decoders. Favor a context-primary or reductive account if every alleged enlargement factors through extra data, compute, retrieval, or evaluator information, or if the classification has no predictive advantage over ordinary risk and complexity measures.

## Relationship classification

`analogy`. Hypothesis spaces, optimization, representations, readouts, and generalization provide a close structural model for the six diagnoses, but current evidence establishes neither equivalence nor reduction. The analogy earns value only if its invariants and intervention predictions survive real tests.
