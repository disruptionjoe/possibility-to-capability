## Steelman thesis

Possibility to Capability is really an attempt to build a semantic data-processing discipline: a receiver should distinguish what a theory permits, what transformations occur, what correlations persist, what becomes decodable or controllable, and what enlarges an achievable task region—without manufacturing information, agreement, or closure during translation.

## Summary of understanding

In information-theoretic terms, possibility resembles an admissible alphabet or model family; dynamics a channel or transition law; records persistent correlations; access the availability of a decoder, side information, or control interface; and capability a resource-constrained achievable region, not the mere observation of one more symbol. Candidate finality asks whether a settlement remains non-reopenable under an independently specified class of extensions. These are overlapping diagnostic types, not successive times. The repository’s strongest present result is therefore an instrument: source/receiver separation, explicit construction branches, cumulative gates, and failure-preserving classifications. Its positive validation is synthetic. GU-001 is blocked at provenance, so it contributes motivation but no receiver-side physical evidence, method count, capability verdict, or finality verdict.

## Strongest insight

“Information exists,” “a record exists,” “an agent can decode it,” and “the system can perform a larger class of tasks” are different claims. Shannon theory makes this separation natural: correlation is not accessible information, accessible information is not channel capacity, and capacity is defined only relative to a channel class, constraints, error criterion, and coding resources. The repo’s packet discipline adds an important epistemic analogue of the data-processing inequality: receiver-side transformation cannot legitimately strengthen source claim status or conjure independent evidential units.

## Strongest criticism

The capability diagnosis may be carried by the normalization frame rather than by the physical case. A capacity or achievable region changes when one changes the task alphabet, cost constraint, block length, error tolerance, side information, feedback, shared randomness, or decoder. “Holding access and resources fixed” is not neutral until an operational equivalence class proves which variations count as mere recoding. Without such a quotient, access and capability can be separated or collapsed by construction. Finality is even less identifiable: any finite transcript can usually be embedded in a larger channel or continuation unless the admissible extension class is independently justified.

## Hidden assumptions

The framework assumes that source meanings survive packetization; that materially equivalent representations can be identified without circularity; that cross-case task vocabularies and resource budgets admit comparison; that records and decoders can be separated; that factorization searches have defensible coverage; and that the chosen continuation class is neither too weak to test finality nor so broad that nothing can ever settle. It also assumes the six-type vocabulary compresses cases without erasing source-specific distinctions needed for prediction.

## Rose

Its current strength is disciplined non-inference. `BLOCKED`, null, construction fork, incomparability, and `HIERARCHY_REVISION` are preserved rather than decoded as favorable signals. GU-001 demonstrates this procedural virtue precisely because the receiver stops before physical interpretation.

## Bud

The most promising development is to replace verbal normalization with operational equivalence: define cases by their achievable decision or control regions under preregistered resource constraints, then quotient reversible recodings and explicitly order information structures by garbling or simulation. That could make the access-capability boundary a testable invariant rather than a persuasive choice of words.

## Thorn

The most dangerous failure mode is a lossy universal codec: heterogeneous theories are translated into six familiar labels, synthetic tests confirm that the labels remain internally distinguishable, and the compression loss is mistaken for explanatory unity. Repeated source-specific forks would then favor plurality, not a more elaborate codebook.

## Confidence

8/10 that this is the best interpretation of what the repository is about. The artifacts strongly support an error-controlled communication and classification layer; the score concerns that framing, not the truth, completeness, novelty, or physical universality of the hierarchy.

## Suggested experiment

Run a preregistered factorial test on one accepted real packet. Encode the same source-certified physical content in two losslessly intertranslatable representations. Within each, independently vary decoder/access resources while holding the declared channel and task criterion fixed, then vary the feasible transformation/code family while holding access fixed. Blind classifiers should diagnose the first intervention as access-only and the second as capability change, with the same verdict across encodings. Disagreement is evidence for construction dependence or operational-context primacy.

## Suggested theorem or mathematical direction

Model packets and receiver operations as provenance-bearing channels and diagnoses as a functor into a partially ordered outcome space. Seek a theorem giving (i) invariance under reversible sufficient recodings, (ii) monotonic non-upgrading of source claims under receiver-only processing, and (iii) capability monotonicity in a resource-constrained achievable-region order. Counterexamples should become formal construction forks or hierarchy revisions, not exceptions hidden in prose.

## Suggested falsification test

Find two source-certified operationally equivalent representations—same decision problems, costs, errors, and achievable region—that receive different diagnoses. One robust example would falsify the representation-respecting classifier as presently constructed. Likewise, if every admissible normalization makes an access intervention inseparable from capability change, the access-capability boundary fails for that domain.

## Relationship classification

`analogy`. Source coding, channels, records, decoders, side information, achievable regions, data processing, and sufficient statistics illuminate the proposed distinctions, but the repository has not yet supplied probability spaces, channel laws, asymptotic regimes, or quantitative invariants that would justify homology, reduction, or equivalence. Finality especially has no established Shannon-theoretic counterpart.
