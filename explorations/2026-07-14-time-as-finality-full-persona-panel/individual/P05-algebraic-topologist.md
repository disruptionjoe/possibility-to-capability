## Steelman thesis

Possibility to Capability is best understood as a proto-obstruction theory for physical explanation: it asks which explanatory distinctions survive admissible changes of presentation and which settlements remain as scoped obstructions after factorization through earlier diagnostic types has been attempted. The arrow names a partially ordered family of tests, not a history of the world.

## Summary of understanding

The repository separates admissible possibilities, dynamics, records, access, normalized capability, and candidate finality because these answer different typed questions. In topological language, frozen packets resemble fixed input objects; construction branches resemble distinct charts or models; admissible reformulations should act like equivalences; and gate results resemble extension or lifting tests. Preserving blocks, nulls, incomparable cases, and `HIERARCHY_REVISION` prevents the classifier from forcing every case into one linear filtration. The present evidence establishes only that this machinery behaves coherently on stipulated synthetic cases and stops correctly at GU-001 provenance. No real packet has completed the physical gate sequence.

## Strongest insight

Finality is treated most fruitfully not as a new substance but as a residual obstruction. A failure to factor through possibility, dynamics, records, access, or capability is analogous to a cocycle not shown to be a coboundary within a declared complex. The repository correctly keeps this bounded: `NO_FACTOR_FOUND` under a named search scope is not proof of absolute non-reducibility, and admissible continuation may kill the apparent class.

## Strongest criticism

The program does not yet define the mathematical structure needed for its hoped-for invariance. There is no specified category of constructions, class of weak equivalences, functorial diagnostic assignment, or coefficient system. Consequently, “representation-respecting” remains an ambition rather than a theorem. In particular, the access/capability normalization may function like an arbitrary choice of coordinates that manufactures the invariant it is supposed to reveal.

## Hidden assumptions

The steelman assumes that source-preserving translations compose; that physically equivalent representations can be identified without circularly using the desired diagnosis; that local diagnoses glue across overlapping constructions; that normalization admits a defensible quotient; and that failures at successive types can be separated rather than arising from one global constraint. It also assumes a finite scoped factorization search can yield scientifically useful obstruction evidence despite never proving absolute non-factorability.

## Rose

Its strongest current feature is honest failure preservation. Construction forks, incomparability, blocking, and hierarchy revision are treated as results rather than noise. That is exactly the posture required before one can search for genuine invariants.

## Bud

The most promising development is to replace the verbal hierarchy with a diagram of objects and maps. If diagnoses descend through a localization by source-certified equivalences, the repository could distinguish coordinate artifacts from stable explanatory structure and give precise meaning to construction-relative versus invariant capability.

## Thorn

The most dangerous failure mode is a polished obstruction calculus with no invariant obstruction: diagnosis changes whenever the presentation, observer, task vocabulary, or normalization frame changes. Then the taxonomy would classify receiver choices rather than physical structure, while its elaborate gates could conceal that dependence.

## Confidence

8/10 that this obstruction-and-invariance reading is the best interpretation of what the repository is about. This score concerns explanatory framing, not the truth of the physics; the physical evidence remains synthetic plus one blocked intake.

## Suggested experiment

After a real packet is accepted, require two source-certified physically equivalent presentations and one materially different construction fork. Freeze the comparison maps before classification. Test whether the equivalent pair yields the same diagnostic object through a commuting zigzag while the genuine fork remains separately represented. Include an access-only intervention and a normalized capability intervention. A stable result would be the first serious evidence that the vocabulary detects structure rather than labels.

## Suggested theorem or mathematical direction

Form a category of frozen constructions with a declared class of weak equivalences. Seek a diagnostic descent theorem: if every load-bearing witness and normalization comparison is natural under those equivalences, then the diagnosis factors through the localized category. A further direction is an obstruction tower whose relative cohomology classes measure failure to extend a case through successive diagnostic factorizations. Any “finality class” must remain indexed by the chosen construction, coefficients, search scope, and continuation class. This is a proposed formalization, not a result supported by the current artifacts.

## Suggested falsification test

Reject this steelman if source-certified equivalent presentations produce different diagnoses, if local diagnoses fail to glue, or if every apparent finality obstruction vanishes under a preregistered admissible refinement. Repeated emergence of the same missing type should trigger `HIERARCHY_REVISION`; systematic domain-specific failures would favor explanatory plurality.

## Relationship classification

`analogy`. The candidate strongly resembles obstruction theory: extension, lifting, factorization, relative residue, and invariance are its natural mathematical metaphors. It is not yet `homology`, reduction, or equivalence because no chain complex, boundary operator, functorial quotient, or representation-invariance theorem has been supplied. The relationship could mature beyond analogy only after such structure is defined and survives real cases.
