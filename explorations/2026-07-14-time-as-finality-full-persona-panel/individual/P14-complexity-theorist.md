## Steelman thesis

Possibility to Capability is really a program for distinguishing extensional availability from resource-bounded power: it asks when a change merely alters representation, reachability, information, or permitted primitives, and when it changes the class of tasks a system can perform. Its deepest proposed move is to treat finality as a carefully scoped non-reducibility claim, not as a temporal endpoint or a name for ignorance.

## Summary of understanding

From complexity theory, the six diagnoses resemble different decision problems over a declared construction. Possibility asks membership in an admissible family; dynamics asks reachability under allowed transitions; records ask whether distinctions persist; access asks what can be queried, observed, conditioned on, or controlled; capability asks whether a normalized task family changes; candidate finality asks whether a settlement resists reduction to the prior mechanisms and reopening by allowed continuation. The arrow is therefore closer to a typed reduction diagram than a linear process. Skips, incomparable branches, promise conditions, and `HIERARCHY_REVISION` are natural rather than exceptional.

The current evidence establishes only that this vocabulary and its safeguards behave coherently on synthetic fixtures. No real packet has been accepted, no real eight-gate run has completed, and GU-001 is blocked at provenance; complexity-theoretic interpretation cannot upgrade those facts.

## Strongest insight

The access-capability distinction is the most consequential insight. Complexity theory routinely separates having an oracle, receiving a better encoding, or exposing a hidden witness from changing the underlying computational resources or problem class. The repository's normalization demand is an attempt to prevent an access improvement from masquerading as a complexity-class enlargement. Likewise, No-Artificial-Success correctly forbids splicing reductions proved under different promises, cost models, constructions, or shared premises into one theorem.

## Strongest criticism

An extensional task set is too coarse unless it includes asymptotic cost. Two systems may compute exactly the same functions while one requires exponential time and the other polynomial time; that is normally a profound capability difference. Conversely, adding a finite shortcut may enlarge raw performance without changing the asymptotic class. The present normalization vocabulary risks hiding the decisive variables: encoding length, uniformity, approximation error, randomness, advice, interaction, energy, precision, and scaling.

There is also a computability problem. Equivalence of constructions, absence of a factorization, and closure under every admissible continuation may themselves be undecidable. A bounded search can support only "no reduction found in this class," never an unconditional lower bound.

## Hidden assumptions

The story assumes that cases admit finite descriptions, a defensible input-size parameter, stable task semantics, and a common cost model. It assumes that representation changes can be separated from resource changes; that access can be modeled without silently introducing an oracle; that the relevant reduction notion is fixed; and that physical equivalence preserves the quantities used by the classifier. It also assumes source packets expose enough uniform family-level structure to support complexity claims rather than only one-instance behavior.

## Rose

The packet, premise-dependency, construction-fork, and one-chain disciplines closely match what reliable hardness arguments require. A lower bound is only as good as its model, quantifiers, promise, reduction, and preserved assumptions. Treating blocked and negative outcomes as results is excellent proof hygiene.

## Bud

Replace a single normalized task set with a complexity profile indexed by resource budget, error tolerance, interaction, and construction. That would let the framework distinguish computability enlargement, complexity-class change, parameterized improvement, query-access change, and constant-factor acceleration without forcing them into one capability label.

## Thorn

The most dangerous failure is normalization by verdict: choosing encodings, admissible continuations, or resource boundaries that make the desired separation true. Then "capability" becomes parameterization rhetoric and "finality" becomes hardness-by-failed-search. Sophisticated gate machinery would conceal rather than solve the circularity.

## Confidence

9/10 that this is the strongest complexity-theoretic interpretation of the repository's purpose. This rates the framing, not the truth, universality, novelty, or physical validation of the candidate.

## Suggested experiment

Preregister a paired real-packet test around one scalable task family. In one branch, add only an observation/query interface; in another, add a genuinely new primitive or resource. Freeze the encoding, input-size parameter, error criterion, cost measure, allowed reductions, and rival access-constitutive model before classification. Test whether independent reviewers recover (a) unchanged complexity profile under access-only change and (b) a proved or tightly bounded profile change under the resource intervention. A blocked or construction-split result must remain admissible.

## Suggested theorem or mathematical direction

Develop a representation-invariance theorem: under uniform polynomial-time bi-reductions that preserve task semantics, resource accounting, promise sets, and witness maps, transition diagnoses are invariant. Then characterize each diagnostic gate as a language or promise problem and locate its decidability and complexity. The likely high-value result is conditional: an access change factors through efficient simulation under the fixed model, whereas a capability change requires failure of that simulation under an explicitly named reduction and complexity assumption.

## Suggested falsification test

Reject or substantially revise the candidate if admissibly equivalent encodings change diagnoses; if access and capability cannot be separated after resource-aware reductions are fixed; if all candidate distinctions collapse to one constraint/reachability problem with equal prediction and lower descriptive cost; or if nontrivial gate judgments are systematically undecidable or intractable without conclusion-entailing certificates.

## Relationship classification

`analogy`. Complexity theory supplies a precise structural analogy among reachability, information access, resource-bounded task power, reductions, and lower bounds. Calling it homology, reduction, or equivalence would require a formal mapping that preserves constructions and verdicts across real accepted packets; the repository has not yet earned that claim.
