# P62 — Bandwidth-Bounded-World Architect

## Steelman thesis

The repository is a theory of explanatory bottlenecks: admissible possibilities do not become usable merely by existing, because dynamics, durable encoding, channel access, and control each impose different cuts before a system acquires operational capability. Finality, if the term survives, is a claim about corrections that cannot traverse any admissible channel or continuation—not just about information that has become costly to recover.

## Summary of understanding

In a bandwidth-bounded world, agents and subsystems never receive the full state. They act through finite-rate, noisy, delayed channels with limited storage and energy. Possibility is the source ensemble or admissible world family. Dynamics transforms it. Records are stable encodings. Access is the channel and decoding relation that makes some distinctions available. Capability is the set or rate region of tasks achievable within declared bandwidth, latency, error, energy, representation, and control constraints. Candidate finality would require a settlement witness plus a bounded demonstration that no admissible continuation or channel can restore the excluded alternative.

This is not a temporal ladder. A channel may expose a latent option without changing the underlying controller. A new code can improve task performance without changing the source dynamics. An actuator channel can enlarge control capability without creating a new record. Several cuts can bind simultaneously, and different network decompositions may yield incomparable diagnoses. The repository’s partial-order semantics and construction-fork discipline are therefore essential.

The evidence ceiling remains low: synthetic controls show that the instrument distinguishes stipulated cases and refuses common false implications. They do not establish a physical rate region or finality. The real GU intake is blocked at source provenance and contributes no substantive gate result.

## Strongest insight

The strongest insight is normalization as a cut-set audit. To claim capability enlargement, one must show that the achievable task region changes after accounting for representation, resources, access, and control. This forces the analyst to identify which bottleneck moved instead of celebrating raw information volume, visibility, or nominal actions.

## Strongest criticism

The current set-valued capability construction is too coarse for bandwidth-bounded systems. Capability depends on joint tradeoffs among rate, delay, error, energy, robustness, and horizon. Two systems may perform the same tasks but at radically different feasible operating points. Holding bandwidth or access fixed can also remove the physical resource whose variation is the phenomenon. A rate region or viability frontier is needed, and it will often be non-additive across channels.

## Hidden assumptions

- A common task alphabet and distortion measure are defensible across constructions.
- Channel resources can be separated from control resources without double counting.
- Encodings judged equivalent preserve task-relevant information and latency.
- The admissible continuation class includes all physically relevant correction paths.
- Cut capacities compose simply enough for a one-chain verdict.

## Rose

The candidate already refuses to equate records with access or raw executability with normalized capability. That is precisely the discipline finite-channel analysis requires.

## Bud

Replace binary task-set comparisons with capability regions indexed by rate, delay, energy, error, and control authority. The existing set construction can remain as a thresholded projection, with transfer conditions stated explicitly.

## Thorn

The dangerous failure is bottleneck erasure: a normalization declares bandwidth, latency, or energy “held fixed” even though their coupling is the actual mechanism, manufacturing a clean capability verdict from an impossible comparison.

## Confidence

8/10. Bottleneck separation gives a coherent interpretation of the repository, while no current evidence shows that this quantitative transfer is physically general.

## Suggested experiment

Use a source-issued packet for a closed-loop control task over a finite-rate channel. Preregister two interventions: improved observation coding with unchanged control region, and increased channel/control capacity predicted to enlarge the achievable task region. Run across several latency and energy budgets with an access-constitutive rival. Blind classification must predict performance frontiers, not merely labels.

## Suggested theorem or mathematical direction

Define capability as an achievable rate-distortion-control region. Prove a cut-set upper bound and sufficient coding/control conditions for genuine enlargement. Then establish when an access improvement is only a reparameterization of the same region and when it moves the frontier. Candidate finality could be indexed by zero residual correction capacity under a named channel and continuation class.

## Suggested falsification test

Find source-certified equivalent encodings with identical achievable regions but different diagnoses, or show that every claimed capability enlargement disappears when the full rate-delay-energy frontier is compared. Either result undermines the current normalization and favors a reductive bottleneck account.

## Relationship classification

**Homology.** The bottleneck pattern can instantiate the candidate’s distinctions through source ensembles, dynamics, encodings, channels, and achievable control regions. The homology holds only when task alphabets, resource accounting, and channel decompositions satisfy explicit transfer conditions; it is not equivalence or reduction.
