## Steelman thesis

Possibility to Capability is really an inference-control system for preventing passage from “a description has a model” to “an operation is realizable,” and from there to “the structure is closed or final,” without explicit, representation-respecting warrants. Its strongest contribution is not a new ontology but a typed discipline for locating exactly where such warrants fail.

## Summary of understanding

The proposed arc separates admissible structures, transformations within an assessed family, persistent distinctions, access to those distinctions, normalized operational task sets, and only then candidate finality. Model-theoretically, these resemble different questions about satisfiability, expansion, definability, interpretation, realizability, and extension. The arrow is correctly treated as a partial diagnostic: a model may realize several types at once, omit some, split across constructions, or show that the signature itself is inadequate. Frozen packets and source/receiver separation try to prevent a receiving language from silently strengthening the source theory. The current evidence establishes synthetic coherence and disciplined blocking, not a result about real physical structures.

## Strongest insight

The deepest insight is that explanatory transitions require preservation and reflection conditions, not verbal resemblance. A changed presentation may reveal a definable relation without enlarging the realized task set; an accessible record need not add a normalized capability; and failure to find an extension in one construction need not mean that no extension exists. Preserving forks, nulls, incomparability, and `HIERARCHY_REVISION` makes the framework capable of reporting non-categoricity rather than manufacturing a unique intended model.

## Strongest criticism

No finite packet, finite test suite, or bounded factorization search can by itself justify global semantic closure. Compactness warns that every inspected finite fragment may behave as closed while an elementary extension or nonstandard model reopens it; Löwenheim–Skolem phenomena warn that first-order descriptions rarely isolate one intended infinite structure. The repository limits itself to `FINALITY_CANDIDATE`, which is appropriate, but “no admissible continuation” remains dependent on a chosen language and model class. If those choices exclude reopenings, finality has been placed in the premises.

## Hidden assumptions

- The selected signature captures every physically relevant distinction.
- Source and receiver vocabularies admit meaning-preserving interpretations.
- The task language and normalization frame do not encode the capability verdict.
- The admissible model and continuation classes are independently characterizable.
- Finite evidence references preserve enough semantics to constrain infinite behavior.
- Equivalent physical presentations correspond to an adequate logical equivalence notion, rather than merely a label swap.

## Rose

The repository treats blockage as information. GU-001 stopping at provenance, with every substantive gate unrun, is a strong example of refusing to infer a semantic result from visible syntax or aggregated artifacts.

## Bud

Develop the typed diagnostic as a theory of interpretations between signatures. For each construction, specify what translations must preserve and reflect: models, definable records, access relations, task realizability, and admissible extensions. This would turn representation-respecting ambition into proof obligations and make construction-relative answers mathematically explicit.

## Thorn

The most dangerous failure is a residual-bucket finality: the chosen formalism fails to express a factor or continuation, and that expressive deficit is redescribed as a property of reality. A close second is normalization-induced categoricity, where fixing the comparison language manufactures agreement.

## Confidence

8/10 that this is the strongest interpretation of what the repository is about. This rates the explanatory framing, not the truth of its physics or the existence of irreducible finality.

## Suggested experiment

Construct pairs of finite and infinite structures that agree on every sentence up to a preregistered quantifier rank, and therefore on every bounded packet witness, but diverge on a global reachability or extension property. Run both through the same frozen diagnosis. Success means the receiver returns underdetermination, a construction fork, or hierarchy revision rather than promoting the finite evidence to finality.

## Suggested theorem or mathematical direction

Define a category of diagnostic theories and admissible interpretations. Prove an invariance theorem: diagnoses are preserved under definitional or Morita equivalence when normalization and witness predicates are transported, while genuine expansions, reducts, and elementary extensions have specified monotonic effects. Then characterize which finality criteria are extension-invariant and which are impossible to certify from finite fragments by compactness.

## Suggested falsification test

Take two source-certified, physically equivalent presentations. If they are definitionally equivalent under the declared translation yet receive different capability or finality diagnoses after all witnesses and normalization data are transported, the representation-respecting candidate fails. Likewise, any preserved elementary extension that reopens a reported settlement defeats that case’s finality diagnosis and favors an earlier-type or construction-relative account.

## Relationship classification

**Analogy.** The candidate and model theory share a structural concern with models, interpretations, definability, realization, invariance, and extensions. No reduction or equivalence is earned: physical records, interventions, resources, and source authority carry empirical and operational content not supplied by model theory alone, and no common derivation establishes homology.
