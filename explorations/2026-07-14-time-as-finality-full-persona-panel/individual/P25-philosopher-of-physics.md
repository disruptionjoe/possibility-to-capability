## Steelman thesis

Possibility to Capability is best understood as an audit grammar for physical explanation: it tests whether apparently different achievements—admissibility, transformation, recording, access, operational power, and closure—are genuinely different explanatory kinds or artifacts of representation, coarse-graining, or context. Its deepest wager is methodological rather than ontological: disciplined typing can show exactly where an explanation succeeds, where it equivocates, and where a residue remains without prematurely reifying that residue as “finality.”

## Summary of understanding

The arrow is not a history of the universe or a causal staircase. It is a partial, construction-indexed diagnostic that asks six different questions of a case. The repository’s work is therefore metatheoretical: it supplies provenance rules, comparison frames, factorization tests, and failure-preserving verdicts by which source-owned physical claims might be compared without being silently strengthened.

From philosophy of physics, the core problem is familiar but important: when do two descriptions represent the same physical possibility, and when does a changed description mark a real change in modal or operational structure? The proposed distinctions try to prevent epistemic access from masquerading as physical capacity and effective irreversibility from masquerading as fundamental closure. Current support is limited to deterministic synthetic tests of the instrument. No real packet has been accepted, no real eight-gate run has completed, and GU-001 is motivation only: provenance blocked, all substantive physical gates unrun.

## Strongest insight

The strongest insight is that explanatory claims have types and cannot be promoted merely by narrative proximity. A record is not yet access; access is not yet normalized capability; failure to find a dynamical factor is not yet irreducibility. This is a powerful antidote to a common pathology in foundational physics: sliding from formal localization, through operational significance, to ontological necessity without a theorem connecting them.

## Strongest criticism

The framework’s decisive notions—physical equivalence, common normalization, admissible continuation, and adequate factorization search—are themselves theory-laden. If those are fixed by the preferred interpretation, the machinery may certify conclusions already embedded in its comparison frame. Pure label invariance is far too weak; substantive dualities, changes of coarse-graining, and empirically equivalent formulations are the real test. Until then, “representation-respecting” is an aspiration, not an established property.

## Hidden assumptions

The framework assumes that explanatory roles can be typed without first settling the ontology; that source meaning survives packetization; that operational task sets admit non-circular comparison; that admissible reformulations and continuations can be specified independently of the desired verdict; and that one coherent argument chain is an appropriate unit of support. It also assumes that residual non-factorization is informative even when bounded by a model class. Any of these may fail by construction or by domain.

## Rose

Its current strength is epistemic hygiene. Blocking, nulls, incomparable branches, construction forks, and `HIERARCHY_REVISION` are successful outcomes rather than embarrassments. The GU-001 stop demonstrates that the repository can distinguish possession of suggestive source material from possession of admissible evidence.

## Bud

The most promising development is a genuine invariance theory for diagnoses across physically equivalent formulations. If the same distinctions survive dual descriptions while principled construction differences remain explicit, the repository could become a useful bridge between interpretive debates that presently talk past one another.

## Thorn

The gravest danger is a refined residualism: “finality” becomes the honorific attached to whatever the chosen dynamical, operational, or constraint vocabulary has not explained. Scoped non-factorization and continuation tests reduce this risk but cannot eliminate it if the rival model class is selected post hoc.

## Confidence

8/10 that this metatheoretical audit of explanatory non-equivalence is the best interpretation of the repository’s purpose. This score concerns framing, not the truth, completeness, novelty, or physical applicability of the hierarchy.

## Suggested experiment

Run one preregistered real-packet study in two source-certified equivalent representations. Before seeing receiver outcomes, independent teams should freeze equivalence maps, rival normalization frames, task vocabularies, factorization families, and continuation classes. Compare diagnoses, intervention predictions, and explanatory compression. A disagreement must remain a construction fork or hierarchy revision, not be reconciled by relabeling.

## Suggested theorem or mathematical direction

Model constructions and admissible reformulations as a category, with a diagnostic functor into a partially ordered space of typed verdicts carrying `BLOCKED`, `INCOMPARABLE`, and revision values. Seek conditions under which capability comparison descends to a quotient by representation, resource, access, and control equivalences. Then prove either an invariance theorem or an obstruction theorem showing precisely why no such quotient exists. Finality candidates should correspond only to scoped failures of factorization plus closure under the declared continuation morphisms—not to absolute terminal objects by definition.

## Suggested falsification test

Reject or substantially revise the candidate if a simpler invariant dynamics-or-constraint model reconstructs every stable distinction and intervention prediction with fewer independent choices. Likewise revise it if source-certified equivalent representations repeatedly change diagnoses, or if real cases require normalization frames that entail their own capability verdicts. Recurrent, domain-stable missing types should count for explanatory plurality or hierarchy revision, not framework expansion by default.

## Relationship classification

**Homology.** Philosophy of physics and the candidate share an underlying structural problem: separating representational equivalence, law-governed evolution, evidential accessibility, operational content, and ontological commitment. The repository operationalizes that problem through packets and gates. The relation is not equivalence—the discipline is broader—nor reduction, because philosophical judgments about admissibility, equivalence, and explanation remain load-bearing inputs rather than outputs of the classifier.
