## Steelman thesis

Possibility to Capability is really an attempt to design a typed intermediate language for cross-theory physical explanation: one that makes category errors, provenance upgrades, and invalid compositions detectable before they become conclusions. Its deepest ambition is not to prove a six-stage physics, but to make “what exactly has been established?” a compositional, representation-respecting judgment.

## Summary of understanding

The six terms behave less like successive events than like non-exclusive types or effects assigned to an explanatory case. Frozen packets are proof-carrying modules: source repositories own their exports, while this repository supplies an importing semantics that cannot strengthen them. The eight gates resemble a staged verifier whose later judgments are unavailable when prerequisites are blocked. Construction forks act like alternative elaborations; nulls, incomparability, and `HIERARCHY_REVISION` are explicit result constructors rather than exceptions to hide.

On this reading, “located is not forced” is a typing distinction: identifying the interface at which a value must be supplied does not construct that value or establish a unique inhabitant. “No artificial success” is a compositionality rule: separately typable fragments do not yield a well-typed whole unless one compatible derivation discharges the shared premises along one construction branch.

Current evidence establishes only that this language and verifier behave coherently on stipulated synthetic fixtures and stop correctly on the blocked GU-001 intake. It establishes no physical inhabitant of the full gate chain and no irreducible finality.

## Strongest insight

The strongest insight is that abstraction boundaries are epistemic machinery. Source sovereignty plus immutable packets prevents a receiver from silently changing a module’s specification; explicit premise dependencies prevent apparent method plurality from being mistaken for independent evidence. This turns common synthesis failures into interface violations that can be named and preserved.

## Strongest criticism

A type system can make invalid derivations difficult while still admitting well-typed nonsense. The crucial judgments—physical equivalence, normalization of capability, admissible continuation, and adequacy of a factorization search—are semantic and potentially theory-laden. If those judgments are supplied by hand, the machinery may relocate the disagreement into annotations while giving it the prestige of formal checking.

## Hidden assumptions

The program assumes that source theories admit a shared intermediate representation without semantic loss; that admissible reformulations can be identified independently of the desired diagnosis; that evidence is sufficiently compositional for one derivation chain to be meaningful; and that capability admits a common task vocabulary and normalization. It also assumes bounded failure to factor can be reported without drifting into a decision procedure for an undecidable or open-ended semantic property. Finally, it assumes “finality” can be added as a conservative diagnostic rather than becoming a catch-all top type.

## Rose

Failure preservation is excellent language design. `BLOCKED`, `FAIL`, `INDETERMINATE`, construction forks, and hierarchy revision prevent partial information from being coerced into a Boolean success. GU-001’s terminal provenance stop is therefore genuine evidence that the boundary discipline works, though not evidence for the physical story.

## Bud

The promising next development is a formal semantics for the diagnostic language: source-language front ends, a small typed core, explicit evidence terms, and a receiver semantics. That would let the repo state and test preservation, noninterference, and representation-invariance properties rather than relying mainly on disciplined prose and validators.

## Thorn

The most dangerous failure is semantic laundering: source-specific distinctions are erased during translation, the normalized capability frame encodes the verdict, or the “admissible” search space excludes rival explanations. The resulting derivation could be internally valid while its translation is unfaithful.

## Confidence

8/10 that this typed-interface and proof-discipline account is the best interpretation of what the repository is about. This rates the framing, not the truth, completeness, novelty, or physical universality of the hierarchy.

## Suggested experiment

After a real source-issued packet exists, have two independent teams build separate front ends from two source-certified equivalent representations into the same receiver language. Preregister the translation rules, normalization frames, and rival forecasts; blind the classifiers; then compare gate traces and diagnoses. Agreement supports representation-respecting abstraction. Disagreement should remain a construction fork or trigger hierarchy revision, not be reconciled after inspection.

## Suggested theorem or mathematical direction

Prove a preservation-and-noninterference theorem for the gate calculus: any accepted source equivalence preserves receiver diagnosis; receiver elaboration cannot strengthen source claim status; and a downstream pass implies an explicit, single-chain derivation consuming every prerequisite evidence term. A logical-relations or categorical semantics could characterize when two source presentations are observationally equivalent at the diagnostic interface. A separate conservativity theorem should show that adding `FINALITY_CANDIDATE` creates no new earlier-layer passes.

## Suggested falsification test

Present two source-certified physically equivalent encodings of one real case. If every faithful translation yields different diagnostic types, or agreement requires deleting a load-bearing source distinction, the shared typed intermediate language has failed. Repeated failures across domains would favor explanatory plurality; a simpler invariant constraint semantics reproducing every distinction would favor reduction.

## Relationship classification

**Homology.** The relationship is structural, not merely illustrative: programming-language theory and this candidate both govern composition across sovereign modules by means of interfaces, typed judgments, translation, and invariance under representation change. It is not yet equivalence or reduction because the repository lacks a formal syntax and semantics, and passing its contracts does not establish physical truth.
