# P33 — Cognitive Scientist

## Steelman thesis

The repository is a cognitive instrument for preventing category errors in explanation. It turns a seductive narrative—possibility becoming finality—into a typed set of questions about admissibility, change, memory, availability, agency, and closure, with explicit permission for the conceptual scheme itself to fail.

## Summary of understanding

The six terms organize distinct explanatory commitments. Possibility asks what alternatives a model admits. Dynamics asks what transformations occur within a chosen family. Records concern stabilized distinctions. Access concerns what can be retrieved, observed, conditioned upon, or controlled. Capability concerns the normalized task set, not raw availability. Candidate finality concerns a settlement only after named earlier explanations and admissible continuations have been tested. The arrow is therefore a partial diagnostic, not a temporal script or ontology.

The current evidence supports only instrument behavior: deterministic synthetic cases remained mechanically distinguishable and included nulls, forks, incomparability, and hierarchy revision. No real frozen packet or full real gate run exists. The blocked GU preflight is evidence that the provenance stop worked, not evidence for the candidate story.

## Strongest insight

Its deepest insight is metarepresentational. Many disputes persist because the parties answer different questions while sharing one word: “possible,” “known,” “available,” “able,” or “settled.” A typed diagnostic can reveal the illicit inference from encoded to accessible, accessible to usable, usable to newly capable, or irreversible to final. That is a genuine improvement in reasoning even if every case later reduces to dynamics.

## Strongest criticism

The types may reflect human conceptual preferences rather than joints in the world. Their apparent clarity can result from curated examples and stipulated witnesses. In real reasoning, task framing, causal granularity, agent boundaries, and pragmatic goals determine which distinctions matter. A taxonomy that requires repeated expert judgment may reproduce those judgments instead of constraining them.

## Hidden assumptions

The program assumes that independent reasoners can recover the same types from source-preserving descriptions; that paraphrase and representation changes do not shift classification; that the type boundaries support counterfactual predictions; and that a common task vocabulary can be specified without importing an agent’s purposes. It also assumes that the six-way organization is cognitively usable without encouraging a linear-stage mental model.

## Rose

The best feature is institutionalized self-correction. `UNKNOWN`, `CONTESTED`, construction forks, blocking, and `HIERARCHY_REVISION` are legitimate outputs. This reduces the pressure to assimilate every case to an attractive conceptual arc.

## Bud

The promising next step is to test the taxonomy as a reasoning aid. If trained users make fewer category errors, preserve source boundaries better, and generate more discriminating interventions than users given rival frameworks, the repository would demonstrate practical epistemic value without claiming a new ontology.

## Thorn

The most dangerous failure is vocabulary capture: once researchers learn the six labels, they may redescribe cases until the distinctions appear present. The framework would then increase confidence and fluency while decreasing sensitivity to omitted concepts.

## Confidence

9/10 that the repository’s strongest present story is an error-correcting explanatory grammar. This is not confidence that the grammar is complete, psychologically natural, physically real, or superior to its rivals on real cases.

## Suggested experiment

Run a preregistered, blinded classification study. Give domain experts and trained generalists source-preserving case packets spanning physics, biology, learning, and control, including adversarial paraphrases and representation-equivalent versions. Compare the six-type diagnostic against reductive, context-primary, constraint-first, and domain-specific prompts. Measure inter-rater stability, source-claim leakage, category-error rates, prediction of intervention outcomes, and appropriate use of blocked or hierarchy-revision verdicts. Hold out cases when refining the coding manual.

## Suggested theorem or mathematical direction

Formalize the vocabulary as a partial type system with explicit introduction and elimination rules for each diagnosis. Use countermodels to prove independence among record, access, and capability, and derive minimal conditions under which no total order can represent all valid judgments. A formal-concept lattice or category of construction-indexed diagnoses could express co-location, incomparability, and meaning-preserving translations.

## Suggested falsification test

Abandon or substantially revise the grammar if trained judges cannot classify real packets more reliably than rival prompts, if equivalent paraphrases systematically reverse diagnoses, or if the framework adds no predictive or error-correcting value beyond ordinary domain analysis. A stable omitted category across cases should trigger `HIERARCHY_REVISION`, not a forced relabeling.

## Relationship classification

`analogy`. The repository behaves like a structured mental model and debiasing aid, but that does not make its physical categories reducible to cognitive organization. The relation remains analogical until empirical work shows how the framework changes reasoning and whether that change tracks better explanations.
