## Steelman thesis

Possibility to Capability is best understood as an attempt to build a representation-respecting calculus of explanatory change: it asks whether a physical transition changes the model family, its dynamics, its durable records, its operational access, or its normalized task capacity. “Finality” is not posited as a new force or stage, but retained only as a scoped obstruction to factoring a settlement through those prior types or reopening it by admissible continuation.

## Summary of understanding

The repository is constructing a typed partial diagnostic, not a history of how reality unfolds. Mathematically, its six labels should be read as candidate regions in a space of explanations, with cases allowed to occupy several regions, skip regions, split across constructions, remain incomparable, or expose a missing type. Frozen packets and receiver-side gates are an epistemic analogue of well-posed boundary data: they prevent a synthesis from changing the source problem while solving it. At present, however, the machinery has only survived deterministic synthetic tests. No real frozen packet has been accepted, no real eight-gate physical run has completed, and GU-001 is motivation blocked at provenance.

## Strongest insight

The deepest insight is that many apparent physical disagreements are actually type errors. A new observable is not necessarily a new state; a record is not necessarily accessible; new access is not necessarily new capability; irreversibility is not finality. Treating these as distinct questions creates a disciplined factorization problem: through which structure does the observed change factor, and what invariant residue remains after admissible reformulation? This is especially valuable in gauge theory and relativity, where descriptive enlargement, gauge fixing, boundary structure, global observables, and genuine physical degrees of freedom are routinely confused.

## Strongest criticism

The proposed distinctions are not yet mathematical invariants. “Same representation,” “fixed resources,” “same access,” “common task vocabulary,” and “admissible continuation” all require independently justified equivalence relations or morphism classes. If those choices are theory-laden, the normalization can manufacture the access/capability boundary, while the chosen factorization class can manufacture a finality residue. A sufficiently strong constraint-first formulation may also recover all six diagnoses as projections of one solution object, making the hierarchy useful bookkeeping but not independent explanatory structure.

## Hidden assumptions

The program assumes that physical models admit a tractable groupoid or category of admissible reformulations; that source meanings survive transport across interfaces; that operational task sets can be compared after quotienting descriptive, resource, access, and control differences; that records and access can be separated without presupposing an observer decomposition; and that “no factor found” can be made informative without confusing bounded search with impossibility. It also assumes that one-chain evidential discipline does not exclude legitimate support assembled through genuinely compatible local arguments.

## Rose

Its strongest current feature is failure preservation. `BLOCKED`, construction forks, incomparability, nulls, and `HIERARCHY_REVISION` are treated as results rather than inconveniences. The GU-001 stop demonstrates that the provenance firewall is operational, even though it provides no physical support for the candidate.

## Bud

The most promising development is to replace the verbal hierarchy with a functorial diagnostic: specify model objects, admissible morphisms, task-set assignments, and factorization witnesses so that each diagnosis has a precise invariance claim. That would turn “representation-respecting” from an aspiration into a theorem-shaped requirement.

## Thorn

The greatest danger is an elegant taxonomy whose verdicts are fixed by hidden construction choices. If normalization frames and continuation classes are selected after the case is understood, capability and finality become residual labels rather than discoveries.

## Confidence

9/10 that this is the best interpretation of what the repository is about. This rating concerns the repository’s intellectual aim, not the truth, completeness, novelty, or physical reality of the hierarchy.

## Suggested experiment

Use one real source-issued packet for a finite-dimensional gauge or constrained control system with two source-certified physically equivalent formulations and at least two materially admissible constructions. Before classification, freeze the equivalence maps, task vocabulary, resource/access normalization, continuation class, and rival predictions. Blind independent teams should compute the diagnoses. Agreement across equivalent formulations, coupled with principled disagreement only across genuinely different constructions, would be meaningful progress; disagreement under certified equivalence would force revision.

## Suggested theorem or mathematical direction

Model physical descriptions as a category fibered over physical equivalence classes. Define records, access relations, and normalized task sets as functors or indexed structures, and diagnoses as predicates invariant under equivalences. Seek either: (i) a representation-invariance theorem for the diagnostic under a stated class of gauge/field redefinitions; (ii) a uniqueness theorem for the capability quotient; or (iii) a no-go theorem showing that no such quotient exists when access is constitutive. Candidate finality could then be formulated as a relative obstruction to factorization, explicitly indexed by the admissible morphism and continuation classes.

## Suggested falsification test

Reject or substantially revise the candidate if a source-certified physical equivalence changes diagnosis, if no non-circular capability quotient survives rival normalization frames, or if a single invariant constraint object reproduces every discrimination with fewer assumptions. Repeated real `HIERARCHY_REVISION` outcomes concentrated by domain would favor explanatory plurality.

## Relationship classification

**Homology.** Mathematical physics contains recurring structures genuinely similar in organization—not merely surface analogy—especially quotients by gauge equivalence, functorial transport, constrained dynamics, observable algebras, and obstruction-to-extension problems. But no reduction or equivalence is established: the repository has not yet defined the required mathematical objects, and its physical applicability remains untested.
