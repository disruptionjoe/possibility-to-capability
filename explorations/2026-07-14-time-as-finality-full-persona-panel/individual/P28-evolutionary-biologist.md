# P28 — Evolutionary Biologist

## Steelman thesis

The repository is building a disciplined way to distinguish the exposure of already-admissible variation from changes in the repertoire of adaptations a system can actually realize. In evolutionary terms, it asks when selection merely moves a population through an existing fitness landscape, when history changes access to that landscape, and when the landscape or reachable phenotype set itself has changed.

## Summary of understanding

Possibility corresponds loosely to admissible genotypes, phenotypes, ecological states, or developmental trajectories. Dynamics covers mutation, recombination, selection, drift, development, and population change within a declared family. Records include inherited sequence, epigenetic marks, developmental canalization, ecological inheritance, and other persistent traces. Access concerns which existing variants or phenotypes become reachable or exposed under migration, recombination, environmental change, or altered regulation. Capability is the normalized set of viable tasks or phenotypic achievements after representation, resources, access, and control are held fixed. Candidate finality is a much stronger claim: a settlement that survives a scoped attempt to explain it through those earlier resources and cannot be reopened by an admissible continuation.

The implemented support is synthetic and diagnostic. Thirty-five stipulated cases show that the vocabulary can preserve distinctions, forks, nulls, and hierarchy revision. No real frozen packet has completed the physical gate chain, and the GU case is motivation rather than evidence here.

## Strongest insight

The most valuable distinction is between access to standing variation and enlargement of evolvability. Environmental exposure, migration, or regulatory release may reveal a phenotype already available in the assessed genotype-development map; horizontal gene transfer, a new developmental module, or a changed genotype-phenotype map may alter the reachable repertoire. Evolutionary explanation often slides between these. A forced normalization frame makes that slide visible.

## Strongest criticism

Evolution rarely supplies a fixed admissible family against which change can be measured. Organisms construct niches, developmental systems alter the production of variation, and ecological feedback changes the fitness landscape while populations traverse it. The boundary between access and capability may therefore be an artifact of where the analyst freezes the organism-environment system. A sufficiently inclusive eco-evolutionary dynamics may absorb every higher diagnosis.

## Hidden assumptions

The candidate assumes that task or phenotype sets can be compared across changing environments; that viable equivalence classes survive changes of genetic representation; that a common normalization does not already encode the verdict; and that continuation classes can be specified without excluding the evolutionary innovations most likely to reopen a settlement. It also assumes that selection, inheritance, and environmental construction can be typed separately often enough to be useful.

## Rose

Failure preservation is a genuine strength. Construction-dependent diagnoses, incomparable phenotype sets, blocked provenance, and missing types remain results instead of being redescribed as progress toward a preferred arc.

## Bud

The most promising development is a multiscale notion of capability based on reachable viable phenotypes under explicit mutation, developmental, ecological, and time budgets. That could separate short-run plasticity, standing-variation response, and genuine changes in evolvability.

## Thorn

The gravest danger is treating a convenient fitness landscape as fixed and then declaring every later change to be access or capability relative to that analyst-created background. The taxonomy would appear precise while concealing the evolving state space that matters most.

## Confidence

7/10 that this is the best evolutionary interpretation of the repository’s purpose. This score concerns the steelman’s fit to the research program, not the truth, universality, or novelty of its physical claims.

## Suggested experiment

Preregister paired evolutionary cases with source-issued frozen evidence: one in which an intervention exposes standing variation without changing the genotype-phenotype map, and one in which an intervention plausibly adds reachable viable functions, such as a well-characterized horizontal-transfer event. Blind the receiver to the intended labels. Compare diagnoses under organism-only, organism-environment, and multigenerational constructions, with explicit population size, mutation supply, resources, and time horizons. A branch-sensitive result is acceptable; disagreement must not be averaged away.

## Suggested theorem or mathematical direction

Define normalized evolutionary capability as a quotient of reachable viable phenotype sets under genotype relabelings and phenotype-preserving developmental maps. Prove sufficient conditions for invariance under coarse-graining, and characterize when adding mutation operators, recombination structure, or developmental degrees of freedom strictly enlarges the viability kernel rather than merely changing hitting times within it.

## Suggested falsification test

Reject or revise the distinction if every apparent capability enlargement in well-specified cases factors through standing possibilities plus expanded time, population, environment, or access; or if physically equivalent genotype-phenotype representations produce different diagnoses with no invariant quotient. Repeated dependence on arbitrary system boundaries should favor an eco-evolutionary reductive account or `HIERARCHY_REVISION`.

## Relationship classification

`analogy`. Fitness landscapes, heritable records, accessible variation, evolvability, and adaptive lock-in furnish a powerful structural comparison, but the repository has not established a shared evolutionary mechanism, a formal reduction, or an equivalence. Calling the relation homology would outrun the present evidence.
