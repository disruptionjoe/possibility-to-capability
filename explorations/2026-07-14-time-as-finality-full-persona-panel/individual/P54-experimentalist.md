# P54 — Experimentalist

## Steelman thesis

The repository is a prototype scientific instrument for discriminating what changed in a physical intervention, with unusually explicit calibration controls for provenance, representation, access, normalization, and premature closure. Its real story will begin only when blinded real trials show that the instrument distinguishes cases better than simpler accounts.

## Summary of understanding

The candidate offers six nonexclusive diagnoses rather than six stages. A source-issued frozen packet must pass provenance before receiver interpretation. Construction branches stay separate; one coherent chain must satisfy the gates; blocked, indeterminate, null, and revision outcomes survive. The 35-case synthetic suite is a calibration set with stipulated witnesses, not an experiment on nature. No real packet has been accepted and no real physical gate has run. GU-001 is correctly stopped at provenance, so it supplies no experimental evidence for capability or finality.

Operationally, the most testable boundary is access versus capability. An access intervention changes what can be observed, conditioned, reached, or controlled. A capability intervention must change the normalized task set after the declared description, resources, access, and control frame is held common. Candidate finality is higher risk: it requires a settlement witness, a preregistered factorization search, and an admissible-continuation challenge.

## Strongest insight

The strongest insight is the use of distinct negative controls. A persistent trace need not be accessible; greater raw task availability need not survive normalization; irreversibility need not be finality. Those separations are experimentally meaningful because each can be probed by paired interventions rather than inferred from vocabulary.

## Strongest criticism

The instrument lacks a measurement model. Evidence references are checked for presence, not validity; witnesses are stipulated; reviewer agreement and sensitivity to noise are unknown. “Hold access and control fixed” is easy to state but hard to realize. Without operational definitions, blinding, uncertainty propagation, and baseline comparison, the receiver may classify its own annotations rather than the physical system.

## Hidden assumptions

The program assumes frozen packets capture tacit calibration choices; interventions can vary one diagnostic factor without changing the others; task success has measurable criteria; equivalent representations can be tested; and factorization teams search comparable model classes. It also assumes blocked packets are included in feasibility estimates rather than silently replaced by tractable cases.

## Rose

The current strength is its stop discipline. The GU preflight did not proceed from suggestive material to downstream claims. In experimental practice, refusing to analyze an uncalibrated sample is a substantive quality-control success.

## Bud

The most promising development is a preregistered paired-intervention trial with raw data and source-side metadata frozen before receiver labels are assigned. That would test the load-bearing access/capability distinction without waiting for finality.

## Thorn

The greatest danger is experimenter degrees of freedom hidden in normalization and factorization scope. A favorable result can be produced by choosing the common task vocabulary, excluded resources, or admissible continuations after seeing the data.

## Confidence

9/10 that “uncalibrated but well-designed diagnostic instrument” is the best experimental interpretation. The rating is about the project’s current identity, not the truth of any physical hierarchy. Real-case validity remains entirely open.

## Suggested experiment

Use a controllable physical platform with fixed hardware and preregistered tasks. Intervention A improves state readout while leaving dynamics and actuators unchanged; predict access change with equal normalized capability. Intervention B changes an internal dynamical constraint while restoring identical readout, control interface, and resource budget; predict a strict task-set change if capability genuinely changes. Blind source teams to receiver labels, freeze raw data and calibration, obtain two rival normalization frames, and compare against dynamics-only and context-relative forecasts.

## Suggested theorem or mathematical direction

Develop an identifiability and power analysis for the paired design. Specify which intervention distributions distinguish access-only from capability change under measurement noise, model misspecification, and imperfect normalization. A useful theorem would give conditions under which the two diagnoses are statistically nonidentifiable, forcing `INDETERMINATE` rather than a preferred label.

## Suggested falsification test

Falsify the instrument if blinded reviewers cannot recover diagnoses above a preregistered baseline; if equivalent reformulations alter results; if normalization choices explain more variance than interventions; or if the six-type model adds no predictive accuracy or compression over a simpler rival. Repeated inability to obtain conforming real packets also counts against program feasibility.

## Relationship classification

`analogy`. The repository behaves like an experimental instrument with calibration, controls, and failure states, but it is not yet an experimental method validated on real systems. Homology, reduction, or equivalence would require demonstrated correspondence between gate outputs and reproducible measurements.
