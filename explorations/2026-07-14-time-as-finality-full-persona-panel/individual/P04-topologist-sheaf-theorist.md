## Steelman thesis

Possibility to Capability is really a disciplined local-to-global program: it asks when source-owned, context-local descriptions descend to a coherent global diagnosis, and when apparent novelty is only a newly accessible restriction of already-admissible structure. Its deepest rule is that an obstruction may locate the failure of gluing without selecting a unique global extension.

## Summary of understanding

The six terms are better read as types of section data than as stages. Possibility specifies admissible local sections; dynamics gives lawful transport; records are distinctions stable under relevant restrictions; access specifies which sections can be observed or acted through; capability concerns the normalized sheaf of executable tasks, not merely the opening of a larger observational cover. Candidate finality is the much stronger claim that a settlement survives declared factorization, refinement, completion, and continuation attempts. Frozen packets preserve local provenance; receiver gates test compatibility and descent without rewriting the local theories. This is presently a formal research instrument: 35 synthetic cases behaved coherently, but no real packet has been accepted and no physical eight-gate run has occurred.

## Strongest insight

The access/capability distinction has an exact sheaf-theoretic steelman. Refining a cover can reveal sections that were already present; it does not thereby enlarge the sheaf. Genuine capability change should instead alter the normalized object of operational sections up to the relevant equivalence. Likewise, a nonzero obstruction to gluing can prove that a proposed family has no global section under stated assumptions, but it neither chooses among alternative extensions nor proves that nature instantiates one. This gives Located-Is-Not-Forced real mathematical teeth.

## Strongest criticism

The framework presupposes more common structure than it has earned: a shared base of contexts, admissible covers, overlap maps, restriction morphisms, and an equivalence notion strong enough to compare source theories without semantic loss. Packet identity establishes provenance, not descent. If different sources live on inequivalent sites—or require stacks, higher coherence, or incompatible task semantics—the six labels may merely project a constraint object or conceal irreducible plurality. No real packet yet tests this.

## Hidden assumptions

- Source claims restrict functorially to overlaps.
- A physically defensible Grothendieck topology distinguishes admissible localization from a change of construction.
- Normalized task sets form comparable section objects rather than being constituted by access itself.
- Construction forks can be indexed without privileging one site or cover.
- The chosen obstruction theory detects all relevant failures of extension.
- Persistence under a bounded family of refinements is not silently promoted to absolute finality.

## Rose

Failure preservation is excellent. `BLOCKED`, null, incomparable, forked, and `HIERARCHY_REVISION` outcomes are treated like legitimate failures of existence, uniqueness, compatibility, or descent, rather than patched into a global story. GU-001 stopping at provenance exemplifies the discipline, not physical confirmation.

## Bud

The packet-and-gate machinery could become a genuine descent calculus for cross-theory synthesis: source packets as immutable local objects, interfaces as overlap maps, receiver assessments as compatibility data, and gate results as explicit existence, uniqueness, and obstruction judgments.

## Thorn

The most dangerous failure is circular site design: choosing contexts, covers, normalization, or admissible continuations so that the desired gluing or non-gluing result is built in. Then “finality” becomes the residue of an artificially restricted topology.

## Confidence

8/10 that local-to-global descent is the best interpretation of the repository’s actual machinery. This rates explanatory fit, not the truth, novelty, universality, or physical adequacy of the candidate hierarchy.

## Suggested experiment

Preregister one real packet on a finite cover of overlapping operational contexts. Freeze two source-certified equivalent covers plus one materially different construction. Have independent teams specify restriction maps and normalization before labels are revealed; compute whether local possibility, record, access, and task data satisfy compatibility and glue. Test whether cover refinement changes only access while leaving the normalized capability object equivalent. Any diagnosis change under equivalent covers, semantic failure on overlaps, or branch-dependent result must remain a fork or trigger hierarchy revision.

## Suggested theorem or mathematical direction

Develop a descent-invariance theorem over a category of admissible representations: under site equivalence and source-certified natural transformations, diagnoses should be invariant; access-only change should be characterized by restriction/pullback with equivalent normalized capability sheaves; capability change by non-equivalence of those sheaves. Formulate candidate finality as an obstruction class persistent under a preregistered family of refinements, completions, and continuations, while proving explicitly that such persistence does not select a physical branch or establish absolute non-factorability.

## Suggested falsification test

Find two Morita-equivalent sites for the same frozen physical content. If one yields access change and the other capability change, or one yields candidate finality while the other factors through an earlier type, the diagnostic is not representation-respecting. Repeated need for incompatible sites or higher gluing data absent from the vocabulary would favor plurality or `HIERARCHY_REVISION`.

## Relationship classification

**Homology**, provisionally. The candidate and sheaf theory share a substantive pattern—local data, restriction, overlap compatibility, gluing, extension, and obstruction—not merely a verbal resemblance. But no reduction or equivalence is earned until the repository specifies its sites, morphisms, descent conditions, and invariance theorem on real packets.
