## Steelman thesis

Possibility to Capability is really a multiscale identifiability program: it tries to separate changes in the admissible model family, transition law, persistent statistics, observation filtration, normalized action set, and residual settlement while preventing effects created by coarse-graining or conditioning from masquerading as new physical kinds. Its deepest ambition is a resolution-respecting diagnostic calculus, not a six-stage history of reality.

## Summary of understanding

From a multiscale-statistical view, the six diagnoses resemble distinct objects in a filtered model. Possibility specifies support or model class; dynamics specifies transition structure; records are persistent sufficient statistics or stable coefficients; access is the sigma-algebra available to an observer or controller; capability is a change in attainable decision or control tasks after a declared normalization; candidate finality is a settlement not explained by those earlier objects within a preregistered search and continuation class.

The arrow is therefore a partial typing relation. A phenomenon may occupy several scales, skip types, split across constructions, remain incomparable, or expose a missing type. Current support is limited to deterministic synthetic discrimination and boundary enforcement. No real frozen packet has been accepted, no real eight-gate run has completed, and GU-001 is blocked at provenance; it supports neither this account nor a rival.

## Strongest insight

The strongest insight is that observability and capability are not interchangeable. Multiscale analysis constantly confronts the fact that a feature can become visible after filtering, aggregation, or basis change without the underlying process acquiring a new degree of freedom. Requiring a common operational normalization before declaring capability change is exactly the right defense against confusing disclosure with enlargement.

## Strongest criticism

The machinery currently lacks a demonstrated reconciliation law across scales. Diagnostics that are clean at one resolution can reverse under aggregation, conditioning, or alternative filtrations through Simpson-type effects, aliasing, non-Markovian coarse-graining, or observer-dependent task definitions. Unless diagnoses commute with physically admissible refinement and coarse-graining, the taxonomy may encode the analyst's resolution rather than a stable distinction.

## Hidden assumptions

It assumes that admissible constructions can be organized into a meaningful refinement relation; that a common task vocabulary and normalization exist across relevant scales; that records can be distinguished from the filtration used to read them; that source-certified equivalent representations preserve enough structure for comparison; and that bounded failure to factor is not merely finite power against long-memory, latent-state, or slow-relaxation alternatives.

## Rose

The repo preserves negative information unusually well. Blocking, nulls, incomparable task sets, construction forks, and `HIERARCHY_REVISION` are retained instead of averaged into a favorable verdict. That is good hierarchical-statistical hygiene: disagreement across levels is data, not nuisance.

## Bud

The most promising development is a scale-indexed version of the receiver contract. Each case would be assessed at preregistered resolutions and filtrations, then reconciled by explicit maps identifying which conclusions are invariant, which are construction-relative, and which appear only after aggregation.

## Thorn

The most dangerous failure mode is manufactured finality. A low-frequency common trend, absorbing approximation, finite observation window, or omitted latent variable can look like irreversible settlement. Calling the residual a finality candidate before adversarial long-horizon and enlarged-state tests would turn lack of statistical power into ontology.

## Confidence

**8/10** that this is the best interpretation of what the repository is about. The packet discipline and typed partial diagnostic strongly support an identifiability-and-reconciliation reading. This score concerns explanatory framing only; the current evidence gives no basis for confidence that the physics is true or universal.

## Suggested experiment

Run a blinded multiscale paired-intervention study on one real frozen packet. Freeze at least three temporal or spatial resolutions, two admissible filtrations, and rival normalization frames. At each level classify record, access, and capability independently; use wavelet localization to separate transient from persistent changes and an error-correction/cointegration model to test whether apparent settlement is a shared slow trend. Publish the full reconciliation table, including forks and blocked cells.

## Suggested theorem or mathematical direction

Model constructions as a poset of filtered probability spaces with coarse-graining maps. Define each diagnosis as an operator into a partial diagnostic lattice. Seek a commutation theorem: under stated sufficiency, lumpability, and task-normalization conditions, diagnosis after coarse-graining equals the image of diagnosis before coarse-graining. Characterize the obstruction when commutation fails; that obstruction may formally distinguish legitimate construction dependence from `HIERARCHY_REVISION`.

## Suggested falsification test

The candidate should be revised if two source-certified physically equivalent multiscale representations yield incompatible diagnoses with no invariant quotient, or if a single preregistered latent dynamical/constraint model explains records, access, capability, and apparent settlement with equal or better predictive compression. Repeated scale-dependent access/capability reversals would favor operational-context primacy or plurality.

## Relationship classification

**Analogy.** Multiscale statistics supplies a precise parallel vocabulary and decisive invariance tests, but no current evidence establishes a structure-preserving mapping between its filtrations, sufficient statistics, and reconciliation operators and the repo's six physical diagnoses. Calling the relation homology, reduction, or equivalence would outrun the synthetic-only evidence.
