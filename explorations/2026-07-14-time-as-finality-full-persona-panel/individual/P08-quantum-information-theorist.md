## Steelman thesis

Possibility to Capability is best understood as an attempted resource-theoretic type checker for physical explanations: it asks whether a reported change concerns the admissible state/process family, a channel within that family, a stabilized information-bearing record, an available decoder or control interface, or a strict change in achievable tasks. "Finality" is then only the residual candidate that survives explicit channel simulation, dilation, recovery, and continuation challenges.

## Summary of understanding

In quantum-information language, possibility specifies systems, states, constraints, and allowed processes; dynamics specifies channels or higher-order transformations; records are persistent correlations deposited in quantum or classical memories; access specifies which instruments, decoders, side information, or controls can use those correlations; capability is the resulting task region under a frozen operational resource theory; and candidate finality is a settlement not reproduced by earlier resources under a preregistered comparison. The arrow is therefore a partial typing relation, not temporal evolution. The repository's actual contribution is disciplined comparison: frozen evidence, source/receiver separation, explicit construction branches, cumulative gates, and preserved null or revision outcomes. Its present support is synthetic only; GU-001 stopped at provenance and supplies no receiver-side physical result.

## Strongest insight

The separation of record, access, and capability is genuinely valuable. Quantum theory sharply distinguishes correlation from recoverable information, recoverable information from locally available information, and available information from an achievable communication, discrimination, simulation, or control rate. A stable environment imprint need not be decodable by the assessed agent; an unlocked decoder can improve performance without changing the underlying channel; and a strict change in a channel-simulation preorder can enlarge tasks even after interfaces and resources are fixed. The vocabulary makes these often-hidden comparison choices auditable.

## Strongest criticism

There may be no observer-independent "normalized capability" left after holding access, controls, side information, ancillas, catalysts, error tolerance, and task semantics fixed. Those choices define the quantum resource theory; they are not removable background. Data locking, entanglement assistance, superactivation, catalytic transformations, and single-shot versus asymptotic regimes can reverse or separate capability judgments. The hierarchy may therefore classify statements relative to an operational category rather than identify distinct physical kinds. Finality is still more vulnerable: any channel-level irreversibility may reopen in a Stinespring dilation or larger algebra, so bounded non-factorization is at most relative closure.

## Hidden assumptions

The scheme assumes that a defensible common task vocabulary exists; that free operations and resources can be fixed without deciding the verdict; that system/environment and record/access factorizations are physically meaningful; that representation equivalence can be certified; that task preorders adequately capture capability; and that the allowed continuations are broad enough to make a finality test non-circular. It also assumes one-chain success is appropriate when quantum advantages can be essentially compositional.

## Rose

The refusal to infer physical success from polished machinery is the current strength. `NOT_YET_IMPORTABLE`, construction forks, incomparability, and `HIERARCHY_REVISION` are treated as results rather than embarrassments.

## Bud

The most promising development is a channel-resource formulation in which every diagnosis is indexed by a declared class of free superchannels, testers, side information, and error regime. That would turn the access/capability boundary from prose into a contestable mathematical object.

## Thorn

The gravest failure mode is normalization laundering: choosing the free operations, continuation class, or subsystem boundary so that access changes disappear, capability changes appear, and unexplained residue is renamed finality.

## Confidence

8/10 that this resource-theoretic diagnostic interpretation is the best account of the repository's aim. This is not confidence that the hierarchy is universal or that irreducible finality exists.

## Suggested experiment

Run a preregistered paired quantum test using source-issued packets. Pair a quantum-data-locking intervention, where a small key radically changes accessible information, with a genuine channel-resource change under the same task family. Freeze the ensemble/channel, allowed instruments and superchannels, key and entanglement resources, one-shot error criterion, and alternative normalization frames before classification. If equally admissible frames disagree on whether unlocking is access or capability and no invariant quotient exists, retain a construction fork or revise the hierarchy; if the distinction predicts different task regions robustly, it favors the candidate.

## Suggested theorem or mathematical direction

Model cases in a symmetric monoidal resource theory of quantum channels ordered by free-superchannel simulation. Define access by an allowed tester class and capability by the complete family of achievable decision or communication tasks. Seek a representation-invariance theorem: mutual free simulation implies identical capability regions, while strict preorder separation is witnessed by a discrimination game. In parallel, prove a finality no-go: in any construction closed under Stinespring dilation and environment extension, nonunitarity or apparent information loss alone cannot establish finality; any surviving closure must be explicitly relative to restricted continuations.

## Suggested falsification test

Reject or substantially revise the candidate if physically equivalent channel representations, tensor factorizations, or complete operational encodings yield different diagnoses; if the access/capability split cannot survive data locking or assistance without verdict-entailing normalization; or if every proposed finality candidate becomes ordinary unitary information flow in an admissible dilation.

## Relationship classification

`homology`. The relation is stronger than analogy because the first five types map to established, structure-preserving quantum-information objects: state/process spaces, channels, memory correlations, tester/decoder access, and resource-constrained task preorders. It is not reduction or equivalence because the mapping is construction-relative and quantum information currently supplies no established counterpart to irreducible finality.
