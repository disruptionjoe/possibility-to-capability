## Steelman thesis

Possibility to Capability is an attempt to build a symbolic coding of physical explanations: a typed factor map distinguishing admissible languages, transformations, stored distinctions, observability, controlled task languages, and only residually candidate finality. Its deepest commitment is not to a ladder but to preserving when a coarse-graining is informative, manufactures an effect, or must be revised.

## Summary of understanding

From symbolic dynamics, the six diagnoses resemble properties assigned after choosing an alphabet, partition, observation map, and admissible shift or control language. Frozen packets fix the source object; receiver gates constrain the coding; construction forks retain inequivalent codings; nulls and `HIERARCHY_REVISION` prevent forced classification. Synthetic evidence shows only that the machinery keeps stipulated distinctions separate. It does not show that any real physical system admits the coding, that diagnoses are invariant, or that finality exists. GU-001 contributes a provenance stop and motivation only: no source-issued packet was accepted and no physical gate ran.

## Strongest insight

The access/capability distinction is unusually sharp when expressed symbolically. Refining an observation partition may reveal more words, distinguish more histories, or improve state estimation without changing the controlled transition language the physical system can realize. That is access without normalized capability growth. By contrast, adding a genuinely reachable controlled word or removing one after observation, resources, and controls are normalized is a capability change. The repo correctly refuses to identify “more visible” with “more possible to do.”

## Strongest criticism

Almost every diagnosis can be an artifact of the chosen symbolic factor. Forbidden words, absorbing components, entropy production, record persistence, and apparent one-way evolution can appear or disappear under partition refinement, embedding dimension, sampling rate, hidden-state extension, or control alphabet. Ordinal-pattern asymmetry and permutation entropy can detect time-directed statistics, but neither alone identifies physical irreversibility, much less finality. The repo has tested label permutation, not invariance under substantive generating partitions, conjugacies, or natural extensions. Until that succeeds on real packets, this is a protocol for a still-unidentified quotient.

## Hidden assumptions

- Source meaning survives the receiver’s finite alphabet and factor map.
- There is a defensible class of physically equivalent partitions and embeddings.
- The normalization frame does not encode the desired capability verdict.
- Stationarity, sampling, and finite-word statistics suffice for the proposed comparisons.
- Observation-induced information loss can be separated from underlying irreversibility.
- The admissible extension class is broad enough that “no reopening” is not engineered.

## Rose

The strongest current feature is epistemic honesty: construction forks, incomparable cases, nulls, blocking, and hierarchy revision are preserved, while irreversibility is explicitly insufficient for finality. The GU-001 provenance stop demonstrates that discipline more convincingly than a receiver-constructed success would.

## Bud

The most promising development is a formal representation-invariance layer using commuting diagrams of physical systems, symbolic factors, and controlled languages. Pure relabeling should become the weakest case of a hierarchy including conjugacy, generating-partition refinement, ordinal embeddings, and hidden-state extensions.

## Thorn

The dangerous failure mode is partition-made ontology: a preferred coding manufactures record, access, capability, or terminality, and the gate machinery then certifies properties of that coding as if they belonged to the physical system. Increasingly polished synthetic validators could conceal the absence of real identifiability.

## Confidence

8/10 that this is the best symbolic-dynamics interpretation of what the repository is about. This rates the framing, not the truth of the physics; the real-case evidence remains absent.

## Suggested experiment

Use one source-issued packet for a controlled laboratory dynamical system with hidden states, sensors, and actuators. Preregister several observation constructions, including coarse state partitions and ordinal-pattern embeddings. Intervention A changes sensor resolution only while plant and actuation remain fixed; it should change accessible words but not the normalized controlled language. Intervention B changes a physical transition or admissible control input while sensor and resource factors are fixed; it should change capability. For both, compare word languages, transition graphs, permutation-entropy rates, and forward/reversed ordinal-word distributions. Finally create an apparently absorbing macrostate and ask whether refinement or a justified hidden-state extension reopens continuation. Retain disagreements as construction forks.

## Suggested theorem or mathematical direction

Model each assessment as a controlled dynamical system with symbolic factor. Seek naturality of diagnoses under conjugacy and explicit monotonicity laws under partition refinement. A useful no-go theorem would show that any finite ordinal-word distribution compatible with an apparently irreversible terminal factor is also compatible, under stated conditions, with an invertible or recurrent hidden-state extension. Then symbolic data alone cannot certify finality; a finality candidate is necessarily relative to a declared admissible-extension class. A complementary theorem should characterize when access refinement leaves the normalized reachable-language functor invariant.

## Suggested falsification test

Freeze two source-certified physically equivalent generating encodings of the same real case. If they produce different record/access/capability/finality diagnoses after all branch metadata is restored, the present vocabulary fails representation-respecting classification. Likewise, if an enlarged shift or controlled transition model reproduces every discrimination with fewer assumptions and equal predictive power, update toward reductive dynamics rather than preserving extra types.

## Relationship classification

`analogy`. The source/receiver construction is strongly analogous to a physical system and its symbolic factors, and the diagnostic distinctions map fruitfully onto languages, partitions, reachability, and time-reversal statistics. But no theorem yet establishes common mathematical structure sufficient for homology, reduction, or equivalence; real construction invariance has not been tested.
