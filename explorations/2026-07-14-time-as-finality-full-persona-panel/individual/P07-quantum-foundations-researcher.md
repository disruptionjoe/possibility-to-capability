## Steelman thesis

Possibility to Capability is really an anti-equivocation protocol for physical explanation: it asks us not to confuse a quantum theory's admissible alternatives, unitary or open-system evolution, decohered records, observer access, operational powers, and genuine outcome settlement. Its deepest wager is methodological, not interpretive: only after those explanatory jobs are typed and adversarially separated does “finality” become a scientifically responsible question.

## Summary of understanding

In quantum foundations, the six diagnoses map naturally—but provisionally—onto familiar fault lines. Hilbert-space states and histories specify possibility; channels, Hamiltonians, and instruments specify dynamics; decoherence, pointer states, and redundant environmental encodings concern records; POVMs, fragments of the environment, communication, and control determine access; resource-bounded discrimination, preparation, feedback, and transformation tasks define capability; and finality asks whether an actual settlement remains after unitary dilation, coarse-graining, boundary conditions, conditioning, and admissible recoherence have received their strongest hearing.

The arrow is therefore not a collapse chronology. Consistent-histories frameworks can fork, relational quantum mechanics can index facts to systems, and Quantum Darwinism can generate redundant records without by itself proving observer-independent single-outcome finality. The repository's real product is a disciplined receiver language that preserves precisely these distinctions, including nulls, incomparable branches, blocking, and `HIERARCHY_REVISION`.

## Strongest insight

The separation of record, access, capability, and finality is unusually valuable for measurement theory. Decoherence explains stable reduced-state structure; redundant records explain a kind of intersubjective accessibility; neither automatically enlarges a normalized task set, nor selects one ontic outcome. Treating those as distinct questions prevents “for all practical purposes” irreversibility from silently becoming collapse, and prevents observability from silently becoming physical agency.

## Strongest criticism

The capability normalization may be least defensible exactly where quantum foundations is most interesting. A task is constituted by an instrument, accessible algebra, reference frame, allowed communication, and control resources. Holding access and control fixed may remove the operational context that gives the task meaning. Contextuality and Bell scenarios further warn that a single counterfactual task vocabulary may combine measurements that cannot jointly be assigned context-independent values. If no invariant quotient exists across admissible contexts, the access/capability boundary is construction-relative bookkeeping, not a general physical type.

## Hidden assumptions

- Physically equivalent representations can be certified independently of the desired diagnosis.
- Records can be identified without already privileging an observer, tensor factorization, coarse-graining, or pointer basis.
- A common task vocabulary survives changes of instrument, context, resources, and accessible algebra.
- “Settlement” has a witness stronger than decoherence, redundancy, thermodynamic irreversibility, or conditioned experience.
- Admissible continuations can be bounded without excluding the very dilation or recoherence that would reopen the case.
- Source-issued packets can preserve interpretive meaning across repositories without the receiver completing an argument the source did not make.

## Bud

The most promising development is to turn the typed vocabulary into a comparison framework across decoherence, Quantum Darwinism, consistent histories, relational accounts, and operational reconstructions. A framework that exposes exactly where those programs agree, fork, or use different equivalence relations could be valuable even if finality always reduces away.

## Thorn

“Finality” could become a prestigious residual label for whatever the chosen factorization search failed to explain. Because no finite search proves non-factorability, an underspecified continuation class or omitted dilation could make ignorance look ontological.

## Suggested experiment

Use a reversible quantum-measurement platform: a system qubit, apparatus ancilla, and controllably enlarged environment. Preregister three constructions—global unitary, consistent-histories coarse-graining, and observer-relative access—and paired interventions that independently vary record redundancy, access to environmental fragments, and feedback/control tasks. Freeze tomography, discrimination, reversal, and conditional-control criteria before classification. Demonstrated recoherence should defeat finality for that construction; persistent records without fragment access should remain records, not access.

## Suggested theorem or mathematical direction

Model cases with quantum combs or process theories plus a resource-theoretic task preorder. Define records as stable broadcast structures, access as available effect algebras/channels, and capability as equivalence classes of achievable tasks under a declared resource-control groupoid. Seek an invariance theorem under operational equivalence and Stinespring dilation. A sharp no-finality result would show that any purported settlement in a purifiable process with retained admissible global reversal factors through dynamics and restricted access.

## Suggested falsification test

Apply the full protocol to several source-issued quantum cases in operationally equivalent Kraus, Stinespring, Heisenberg, and histories formulations. If diagnoses change without changed predictions or resources, the vocabulary is representation-sensitive. If every candidate settlement reopens under an admissible dilation or factors through decoherence, conditioning, boundary conditions, or restricted control, “finality” contributes no independent explanatory type.

## Relationship classification

`homology`. The candidate and quantum-foundational measurement analysis share a structural skeleton: alternatives, transformations, stable records, accessible observables, executable tasks, and the unresolved status of outcomes. This is not equivalence—the mapping is construction-dependent and unvalidated on real packets—and not reduction, because neither framework currently derives the other.
