# P60 — Virtual-Economy Designer

## Steelman thesis

The repository is an accounting system for opportunity: it distinguishes legal economic states, transaction dynamics, durable ledgers, market information and venue access, genuinely implementable opportunity sets, and settlements that resist admissible unwind. The real story is that apparent abundance, visibility, liquidity, and capability are not the same economic achievement.

## Summary of understanding

A virtual economy has an authored possibility space: item types, balances, recipes, transfer rules, and allowed contracts. Dynamics includes production, trade, sinks, sources, auctions, and policy changes. Records are balances, transaction histories, ownership claims, and durable commitments. Access includes price discovery, market visibility, geographic or platform reach, and control interfaces. Capability is the set of economic tasks an actor can actually accomplish under a declared comparison of representation, resources, access, and control. Candidate finality concerns settlement only after rollback, chargeback, compensation, administrative intervention, and alternative continuation are challenged.

This reading makes the candidate a typed diagnostic rather than an economic growth story. A new market screen can reveal existing trades without enlarging normalized capability. Better matching can create access or liquidity effects while leaving production rules unchanged. A new crafting right can enlarge tasks even with identical information. A ledger entry may be durable yet economically reversible through compensation. Several diagnoses can occur together, and different accounting or agent constructions may remain incomparable.

The existing repo work establishes only that these distinctions can be kept apart in synthetic controls. It has not accepted a real packet or completed a physical gate run. GU-001 is a provenance stop and cannot be counted as evidence for any economic or physical analogy.

## Strongest insight

The normalized task-set idea is powerful because virtual economies are full of misleading proxies. More listed items do not necessarily mean more attainable consumption; more nominal currency does not imply more purchasing capability; and access to a market does not imply solvency, liquidity, or incentive-compatible participation. Explicitly naming the comparison frame exposes where the conclusion comes from.

## Strongest criticism

Economic capability is endogenous and non-additive. Prices, congestion, expectations, strategic responses, network effects, and distribution make one actor’s opportunity set depend on everyone else’s actions. Holding resources, access, and control fixed may destroy the mechanism that generates the opportunity. A set-inclusion comparison can miss substitution, risk, timing, welfare, and path dependence. The candidate may need capability correspondences or attainable utility frontiers rather than task sets.

## Hidden assumptions

- Tasks have stable semantics across policy and price regimes.
- Resource normalization is possible without freezing endogenous scarcity.
- Individual and aggregate capability can be compared using the same measure.
- Ledger persistence and economic settlement can be cleanly separated from compensation.
- Strategic responses do not invalidate one-chain causal attribution.

## Rose

Failure preservation is excellent economic hygiene. Empty survivor sets, incomparable task sets, blocked evidence, and construction-relative results are informative rather than embarrassing exceptions.

## Bud

Generalize capability from a set of tasks to a correspondence indexed by prices, other agents’ strategies, risk, and time. Preserve the simpler task-set construction as one branch and require transfer conditions before generalizing.

## Thorn

The most dangerous failure is policy laundering: analysts choose a normalization that calls a preferred intervention “capability enlargement” while hiding redistribution, inflation, or shifted risk.

## Confidence

8/10. Opportunity accounting is a strong disciplinary steelman of the repository, not evidence for its physical candidate.

## Suggested experiment

Run paired, preregistered economy sandboxes with identical production rules and starting endowments. In one, reveal prices or add market search without changing permissions; in another, add an enforceable production or exchange right while holding the declared access channel constant. Freeze full transaction and policy records. Blind evaluators should predict attainable bundles under task-set, utility-frontier, and strategic-equilibrium constructions.

## Suggested theorem or mathematical direction

Define capability as an implementable opportunity correspondence and quotient it by observationally equivalent representations. Prove conditions under which an information refinement changes only access, while a rule change strictly expands attainable allocations for at least one agent without contracting others under the same resource accounting. Where externalities break monotonicity, require a partial order or welfare correspondence rather than set inclusion.

## Suggested falsification test

Find economically equivalent representations or market institutions that yield different capability diagnoses with identical attainable allocation correspondences. If the classifier tracks interface form rather than economic opportunity, the normalization fails. Repeated non-additive cases would justify hierarchy revision.

## Relationship classification

**Analogy.** Virtual economies provide engineered examples of admissibility, records, access, opportunity, and settlement, but endogenous incentives and authored rules prevent assuming a shared mechanism with physical capability or finality.
