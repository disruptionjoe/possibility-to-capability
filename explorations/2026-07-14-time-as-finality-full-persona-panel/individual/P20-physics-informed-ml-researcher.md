# Physics-Informed Machine Learning Researcher — Aisha Rahman

## Steelman thesis

Possibility to Capability is building a falsification-aware type system for deciding whether an apparent advance comes from changing the admissible model family, the evolution law, stored physical memory, observation/control access, or the normalized set of achievable tasks—and for reserving “finality” for bounded failures of all such factorizations. Its real product is not a new physical sequence but a provenance-preserving experimental interface for comparing explanations without converting model success into ontology.

## Summary of understanding

From physics-informed ML, the six diagnoses resemble distinct parts of a predictive system: hypothesis or state space; transition operator; persistent state or memory; observation, conditioning, and control maps; reachable task performance under a frozen resource frame; and a residual settlement test. The important claim is that these objects must not be conflated. A reservoir can contain a predictive trace that no readout accesses; a better sensor or decoder can reveal performance without changing the underlying dynamics; a changed actuator budget can enlarge reachability without establishing an intrinsic new physical capacity. The repository turns those distinctions into a typed partial diagnostic that retains skips, co-location, incomparable cases, construction forks, nulls, blocking, and `HIERARCHY_REVISION`.

## Strongest insight

The separation of record, access, and normalized capability is the most valuable insight. In reservoir computing, latent state, readout, and task score are routinely collapsed into one statement that a system “learned” or “can do” something. Here, persistent encoding, extractability, and intervention competence require separate witnesses. That discipline could prevent both ML benchmark inflation and physical overinterpretation.

## Strongest criticism

The normalization needed to isolate capability may be less physical invariant than evaluator design. Holding description, representation, resources, access, and control fixed can remove exactly the couplings that constitute capability in embodied or closed-loop systems. If admissible frames yield different task-set relations, the result is not a noisy estimate of one capability; it may be irreducibly construction-indexed. The synthetic suite shows contract coherence under stipulated witnesses, not that nature supplies the required quotient.

## Hidden assumptions

- Physically equivalent representations can be identified independently of the desired diagnosis.
- State, memory, observation, control, and task semantics admit separable maps.
- A common task vocabulary survives discretization, coarse-graining, and observer choice.
- “No admissible continuation” can be scoped without excluding the strongest reopening model.
- Frozen packets preserve enough experimental context while remaining source-neutral.
- Diagnostic usefulness will add prediction or compression beyond a complete dynamical model.

## Rose

The repository is designed to lose honestly. Provenance blocking, one-chain evaluation, non-upgrading reception, bounded non-factorization, and first-class construction forks are excellent safeguards against the familiar PIML failure of treating a well-fit surrogate as evidence for the preferred physics.

## Bud

The most promising development is an invariance theory linking diagnoses across state-space models, delay embeddings, reservoir states, and neural-operator representations. If the same intervention receives the same type under source-certified equivalent formulations, the vocabulary could become a useful cross-model audit layer rather than a verbal taxonomy.

## Thorn

The gravest risk is an elegant, ever-expanding validation apparatus with no real accepted packet. A second risk follows: “finality” becomes the prestigious label for residual model error after an insufficient search over latent variables, boundary conditions, memory, or multiscale dynamics.

## Confidence

**8/10** that this is the repository’s best current interpretation. The machinery and ranked tests strongly support the interface-and-diagnostic reading; the evidence explicitly does not support a universal physical arc or irreducible finality.

## Suggested experiment

Freeze a real controlled-fluid packet containing raw trajectories, sensor geometry, actuator limits, task definitions, and two preregistered representations: a physics-informed neural operator and a reservoir/delay-state model. Run paired interventions: change only sensor/readout access, then change physical actuation authority while restoring the same access and resource accounting. Blind reviewers should specify rival normalization frames before outcomes. Test whether access-only versus capability-change diagnoses agree across representations and predict distinct closed-loop task success. Disagreement must remain a construction fork, not be averaged.

## Suggested theorem or mathematical direction

Formalize each case as a controlled dynamical system with record map, observation map, control map, and normalized task functor. Prove a diagnosis-invariance theorem under conjugacy or control bisimulation: if two representations commute with dynamics, records, observations, controls, and task evaluation, their diagnoses agree. Then prove a factorization criterion: an intervention that changes only the observation/readout morphism preserves the capability quotient under stated sufficiency conditions. Failure of those conditions should produce an explicit obstruction or `CONSTRUCTION_FORK`, not a forced capability verdict.

## Suggested falsification test

For every proposed finality candidate, preregister an expanding sequence of rival predictors: Markov state, delay embedding, latent-memory reservoir, multiscale neural operator, enlarged boundary-condition model, and global-constraint formulation. If any source-admissible enlargement predicts the alleged settlement or exhibits a continuation that reopens it, finality fails for that construction. If diagnostic labels vary under certified equivalent representations, the current hierarchy itself is falsified or requires revision.

## Relationship classification

**Homology.** The candidate and physics-informed ML share more than metaphor: admissible state families, evolution operators, memory states, observation/control maps, and reachable task sets are corresponding mathematical structures. The relation is not equivalence or reduction because PIML models are epistemic approximations, capability normalization may be context-constitutive, and candidate finality has no established counterpart in standard predictive dynamics.
