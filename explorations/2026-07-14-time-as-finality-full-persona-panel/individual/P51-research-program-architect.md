# P51 — Research Program Architect

## Steelman thesis

The repository is an architecture for cumulative inquiry across sovereign research programs: immutable source packets enter a neutral receiver, typed gates locate exactly what changed, and failures remain reusable program knowledge. Its real promise is to turn cross-repository synthesis from persuasive narrative into a sequenced, auditable experiment in interoperability.

## Summary of understanding

The candidate hierarchy distinguishes admissible possibility, dynamics, record formation, access, normalized capability, and candidate finality without requiring a linear order. Frozen-Packet v0.2 provides reproducible identity and source/receiver separation. The eight-gate run preserves blocking and rejects split-chain success. Synthetic cases exercise the vocabulary, while the GU-001 preflight demonstrates the provenance stop. The evidence ceiling is exact: zero accepted real packets and zero completed real gate runs. GU-001 is motivation only, not a partial success.

As a program design, the ranked tests are properly dependency-aware. First establish one real source-to-receiver passage. Then test the access/capability hinge, then adversarially factor finality, then challenge representation and construction invariance, and only afterward attempt cross-domain generality. Preparation may fan out, but verdicts remain serialized and owner-specific.

## Strongest insight

The strongest insight is that interoperability is itself an empirical research problem. A shared vocabulary is not achieved by declaring one. Source meaning, construction, grades, and authority have to survive transport, and inability to do so may favor plurality. The packet boundary makes semantic loss visible instead of treating it as coordination noise.

## Strongest criticism

The program has optimized the receiver before proving the supply chain. Upstream issuance is costly, and no real packet has crossed it. This creates a classic platform risk: polishing schemas, validators, and gates while the critical path remains source participation. The program needs a strict work-in-process limit and an explicit criterion for when continued intake failure falsifies feasibility rather than just delaying it.

## Hidden assumptions

It assumes source owners see value in issuing packets; packet cost is proportional to scientific value; immutable freezing does not freeze immature errors; rival reviewers can be recruited independently; and JoeOps sequencing will not bias case selection toward likely passes. It also assumes one-chain evidence is scientifically adequate when some valid results may require convergent but nonidentical arguments.

## Rose

The ranked decisive-test program is the current strength. It puts the first real intake above further synthetic polish and makes later claims depend on earlier evidence rather than on enthusiasm.

## Bud

The most promising development is a minimal viable real packet issued with the smallest source burden that still preserves premises, grades, constructions, and digests. Time and semantic-loss measurements from that run should inform whether v0.2 is appropriately sized.

## Thorn

The worst failure is process capture: packet completion becomes the deliverable, gate compliance becomes success, and the program accumulates perfect receipts without producing a discriminating physical result.

## Confidence

9/10 that the strongest program-level interpretation is an interoperability architecture for falsifiable synthesis. This rating does not endorse the physics. The architecture has synthetic verification and one disciplined block, but no evidence yet of successful real interoperability.

## Suggested experiment

Run a time-boxed Rank 1 pilot with one source owner, one receiver, and one adversarial reviewer. Preregister the minimum packet, allowed clarification loop, labor budget, semantic-preservation checks, gate forecasts, and stop conditions. Track preparation time, validation defects, source-disputed paraphrases, receiver abstentions, and whether any scientific discrimination survives. Maintain a dynamics-only shadow analysis for comparison.

## Suggested theorem or mathematical direction

Model the workflow as a typed state machine with a provenance lattice. Prove safety—that downstream pass states are unreachable after failed prerequisites and source claims cannot be strengthened by receiver transitions—and conditional liveness—that a conforming source object reaches a terminal receiver verdict. Then characterize where one-chain requirements are complete or systematically exclude legitimate evidence structures.

## Suggested falsification test

Falsify the current program architecture if several serious source teams cannot issue conforming packets within a bounded burden, if packetization repeatedly changes scientific meaning, or if terminal gate results do not alter predictions or research choices. A platform that only produces better audit trails should be reduced to an archival tool.

## Relationship classification

`homology`. The candidate’s scientific object and the program architecture are not identical, but they share a structure: typed states, admissible transitions, preserved obstructions, and nonpassing boundaries. This is stronger than a loose analogy yet does not imply reduction or equivalence.
