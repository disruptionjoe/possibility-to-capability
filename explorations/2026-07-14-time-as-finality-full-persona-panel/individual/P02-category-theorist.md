## Steelman thesis

Possibility to Capability is really an attempt to make explanatory diagnoses functorial: a physical case should carry distinctions that survive admissible changes of presentation, while construction-dependent branches remain separate. Its deepest question is whether possibility, dynamics, records, access, normalized capability, and candidate finality define coherent partial structure rather than a linear physical process.

## Summary of understanding

The natural objects are frozen, construction-indexed case presentations; the natural morphisms are source-certified reformulations, admissible embeddings or continuations, and perhaps interventions. The six terms are then probes or partial functors on those objects, not successive moments. Source sovereignty asks the receiver to be conservative: it may classify imported structure but may not create or strengthen source truth. Construction forks form a diagram whose branches cannot be merged unless a legitimate comparison or universal construction exists. Blocking, nulls, incomparability, and `HIERARCHY_REVISION` are therefore mathematical data, not failed observations.

On this reading, capability is a comparison between task-set objects after a declared normalization, and finality is at most a relative universal-property candidate: closure against a specified class of continuations plus an obstruction to factorization through earlier diagnoses. No accepted real packet yet supports such a physical construction; current evidence establishes only synthetic behavior and one correct provenance stop.

## Strongest insight

The prohibition on artificial success is a compositional insight. Local witnesses from different packets, argument chains, or construction branches do not automatically constitute a global witness. In categorical language, compatible-looking local data need not possess a cone, descent datum, or global section. Preserving that failure is more important than the particular six labels.

## Strongest criticism

The repository has not yet specified enough categorical structure to earn its invariance ambition. What are the objects, admissible morphisms, identities, compositions, and equivalences? Are normalization choices natural under those morphisms? Without closure under composition, “representation-respecting” remains a test slogan rather than functoriality. A constraint-first model could also make all six diagnoses mere projections of one object, reproducing every distinction with less independent machinery.

## Hidden assumptions

- Admissible reformulations and continuations form a category, or a controlled higher-category, rather than an ad hoc list.
- Packetization is faithful enough to preserve load-bearing source structure, and receiver maps are conservative.
- The normalization relation for capability is a congruence: equivalent presentations yield comparable task objects without presupposing the verdict.
- Factorization and continuation scopes are compositionally closed enough for a negative result to mean more than search failure.
- The diagnostic types remain stable under base change; otherwise they are construction-local coordinates.
- “Finality” can be represented by a relative terminality, closure, or orthogonality property, although those are distinct notions and must not be conflated.

## Rose

The machinery treats failed gluing honestly. Forks, nulls, blocks, incomparable branches, and hierarchy revision survive instead of being collapsed into a favorable total order.

## Bud

Formalize the receiver as an indexed category or fibration over constructions. This would make source-local truth live in fibers, permitted transport explicit, and cross-construction agreement a theorem or test rather than a narrative expectation.

## Thorn

Normalization may secretly choose the quotient that manufactures capability, while a bounded failure to factor may be rhetorically upgraded into finality. Either move would turn chosen presentation into purported invariant structure.

## Confidence

8/10 that functorial diagnostic coherence is the best interpretation of the repository’s purpose. The score concerns explanatory framing, not the truth of the physics; it remains below 10 because the categorical structure is implicit and untested on real accepted objects.

## Suggested experiment

Issue one real case in three source-certified equivalent presentations with frozen maps (A \to B \to C) and the composite (A \to C). Blindly classify each presentation and require identity, composition, gate status, capability comparison, and scoped finality data to commute up to declared isomorphism. Retain genuinely non-equivalent construction branches separately. Failure of the triangle is more informative than another label-swap test.

## Suggested theorem or mathematical direction

Let \(\mathcal C\) be a category of frozen presentations and \(W\) its certified physical equivalences. Define a partial diagnostic functor \(D\) into a category containing outcomes, evidence dependence, and construction forks. Prove conditions under which \(D\) descends to the localization \(\mathcal C[W^{-1}]\). Treat normalized task sets as a functor to a poset-enriched category, and formulate candidate finality only relative to a named continuation/factorization category. A failure of descent should return construction dependence or hierarchy revision, not be repaired silently.

## Suggested falsification test

Find a source-certified commutative triangle of equivalent presentations for which the two induced diagnostic transports disagree, or where restoring an admissible branch changes capability/finality without a source-grounded distinction. If independent repair cannot recover a coherent functor without conclusion-entailing normalization, reject the cross-presentation diagnostic and favor operational context, plurality, or constraint-first reduction.

## Relationship classification

`analogy`. The candidate strongly resembles categorical practice—typed objects, lawful transport, gluing, factorization, and relative universal properties—but no actual category, functor, or universal construction has yet been established. Calling the relation homology, reduction, or equivalence would outrun the evidence.
