## Steelman thesis

Possibility to Capability is really an instrument for testing emergence: it asks when a change is merely a redescription, trajectory, persistent trace, or newly opened interaction channel, and when it constitutes an invariant enlargement of what a system can do. Its deeper wager is that claims of higher-level capability or final settlement can be made scientifically accountable by forcing them to survive explicit construction, normalization, factorization, and reopening tests.

## Summary of understanding

The six diagnoses resemble questions about an adaptive system's state space, transitions, memory, observation/control channels, viable task set, and apparently terminal selections. The arrow is not an evolutionary sequence but a partial typing system permitting co-location, skipped types, incomparable branches, nulls, and revision. The repository is thus less a theory of emergence than a protocol for testing emergence claims across scales and representations.

Current support is methodological: synthetic cases show that the classifier preserves stipulated distinctions and rejects selected category errors. No real packet or eight-gate run exists; GU-001 is motivation behind a provenance stop, not physical evidence.

## Strongest insight

The access/capability distinction targets a recurrent error: a new sensor, actuator, communication edge, or coarse-graining may enlarge executable interventions without changing normalized viability or reachability. A common frame makes “new capability” a comparative claim. Retaining an access-constitutive fork also admits that adaptive coupling may make normalization fail.

## Strongest criticism

In adaptive systems, the held-fixed context may constitute the phenomenon: agents, tasks, affordances, topology, sensing, and control co-evolve. Factoring access from capability can destroy the measured system. A normalization frame may decide the result, while a “fixed family” suppresses endogenous state-space expansion. Without stability across independently defended coarse-grainings, the framework risks bookkeeping for observer-relative decompositions.

## Hidden assumptions

The program assumes that admissible constructions and equivalences can be identified; records separated from readers; task vocabularies and intervention budgets compared; and factorization searches challenge finality without presupposing it. It also assumes packets preserve the relational context of open systems, scale changes preserve relevant causal organization, and one argument chain can represent evidence that may instead arise from mutually constraining models.

## Rose

Its strongest current feature is disciplined failure preservation. `BLOCKED`, `INDETERMINATE`, construction forks, incomparability, nulls, and `HIERARCHY_REVISION` prevent ambiguity from being converted into emergence or finality. The GU-001 stop is evidence that this discipline is operational, though not evidence for the physical story.

## Bud

The most promising development is a real, preregistered multiscale intervention study that treats representation choice as an experimental variable. If the same access-only versus capability distinction survives several admissible micro/macro descriptions and predicts different interventions, the vocabulary would begin to earn scientific value beyond schema integrity.

## Thorn

The greatest danger is “finality” becoming a prestige label for an unmodeled absorbing regime. Complex systems routinely display metastability, hysteresis, frozen disorder, broken ergodicity, and apparent absorbing states that reopen under longer times, larger state spaces, rare perturbations, or environmental coupling. A bounded failure to reopen must never become ontological closure.

## Confidence

8/10 that this is the best interpretation of what the repository is about. The evidence strongly supports reading it as an anti-category-error and emergence-testing protocol; it does not support the truth, completeness, or universality of its physical taxonomy.

## Suggested experiment

Use one source-issued packet for an adaptive network with a frozen transition model, intervention budget, task vocabulary, and several independently justified coarse-grainings. Preregister paired perturbations: one adds sensing or an actuator while leaving the normalized viability kernel unchanged; another rewires interaction structure so the viability kernel strictly changes under matched access and cost. Blind assessors classify both across all retained constructions. Distinct, representation-stable predictions favor the candidate; unresolved branch dependence favors operational-context primacy or hierarchy revision.

## Suggested theorem or mathematical direction

Model a case as a controlled transition system with an intervention monoid, observation maps, memory variables, and a viability kernel. Define diagnoses on equivalence classes under source-certified bisimulation or lumpable coarse-graining. Seek a naturality theorem: access-only and capability-change diagnoses commute with admissible representation maps exactly under stated sufficiency and control-preservation conditions. Failure to prove this should yield an explicit obstruction or construction fork. Candidate finality could then be represented cautiously as a terminal class under a declared continuation category, not as absolute finality.

## Suggested falsification test

Preregister two source-certified physically equivalent representations of the same real system. If restoring all admissible branches changes whether the case is access change, capability change, or candidate finality, with no invariant quotient and no empirically superior branch, the cross-domain diagnostic claim is falsified or must become construction-indexed. If a single dynamics-plus-constraint model also matches every discrimination with fewer assumptions, prefer that reduction.

## Relationship classification

**Homology**, provisionally. The candidate's types correspond structurally to state spaces, flows, memory/order parameters, observability and controllability, viability/reachability, and terminal classes in complex-systems mathematics. This is not equivalence or reduction: adaptive coupling may collapse the access/capability boundary, and an attractor is not finality. The claimed homology remains a formal research conjecture until real cases survive multiscale invariance tests.
