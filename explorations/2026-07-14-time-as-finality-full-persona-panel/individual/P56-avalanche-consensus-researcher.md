# P56 — Avalanche-Class Consensus Researcher

## Steelman thesis

The repository is an anti-premature-finality protocol for explanation: it separates alternatives, local transitions, durable evidence, query access, operational power, and settlement so that repeated visibility or confidence cannot masquerade as an irreversible physical decision. Its real story is that finality is meaningful only relative to an explicit adversary, continuation class, and prior factorization search.

## Summary of understanding

Avalanche-class protocols begin with competing admissible choices and use repeated randomized subsampling to amplify a preference toward metastable agreement. The important distinctions are not merely “unfinalized” and “finalized.” One must separate the conflict set, local preference dynamics, observations of peers, confidence accumulation, the ability to transact, and the protocol threshold after which reversal becomes sufficiently unlikely under a stated fault model.

The repository’s typed partial diagnostic serves a comparable clarifying role without claiming a consensus mechanism. Possibility names the assessed alternative family; dynamics describes transformation within it; records stabilize distinctions; access makes some distinctions condition later action; capability compares normalized task sets; finality remains only a candidate after settlement, bounded non-factorization, and admissible-continuation challenges. Skips, multi-level diagnoses, construction forks, and hierarchy revision matter because distributed protocols also permit local, probabilistic, and scope-relative achievements rather than one universal ladder.

Current evidence supports only instrument behavior. Thirty-five synthetic cases show that the classifier can avoid several false implications. The real founding intake stopped at provenance. GU-001 therefore contributes motivation and a demonstrated stop condition, not a vote for the candidate or any rival.

## Strongest insight

The strongest insight is that confidence accumulation is not capability enlargement and neither is finality. More observations can improve a node’s belief while leaving its permitted operations unchanged. Conversely, a protocol rule can enlarge executable operations before global settlement. The repository’s access/capability distinction and its refusal to infer finality from irreversibility address exactly these conflations.

## Strongest criticism

“No admissible continuation reopens the case” is underspecified until the continuation distribution and adversary budget are named. In probabilistic consensus, absolute non-reopening is generally the wrong standard; finality is a quantified bound conditioned on network synchrony, sampling assumptions, Byzantine fraction, and time horizon. A qualitative finality candidate risks becoming either impossible to earn or too easy to declare through a narrow continuation class.

## Hidden assumptions

- The assessed possibility family is sufficiently complete to contain meaningful conflicts.
- The continuation class is chosen independently of the desired settlement.
- Normalized capability can be compared while access and control are held fixed.
- Evidence dependencies are known well enough to prevent correlated methods from appearing independent.
- One coherent gate chain can represent support that may naturally be probabilistic and cumulative.

## Rose

The architecture is designed to preserve indecision. `BLOCKED`, `INDETERMINATE`, construction disagreement, and hierarchy revision remain legitimate outcomes instead of being collapsed into a binary verdict.

## Bud

Turn finality into an indexed object: finality candidate relative to continuation class, adversary model, horizon, and tolerated reopening probability. This would make scoped closure comparable without promoting it to metaphysical irreversibility.

## Thorn

The dangerous failure is threshold laundering: a chosen search depth or continuation cutoff creates the appearance of finality exactly as a poorly calibrated confidence threshold creates unsafe consensus.

## Confidence

7/10. The anti-premature-finality reading fits the machinery well, while the physics remains entirely untested by a real accepted packet.

## Suggested experiment

Construct a blinded tournament over simulated conflict systems with identical local records but varied sampling correlation, adversary fraction, latency, and continuation horizon. Ask the diagnostic to distinguish increased access, increased transaction capability, metastable preference, and scoped finality. Preregister rival dynamical and constraint-first explanations. The test should return construction-indexed outcomes when fault models disagree, not average them.

## Suggested theorem or mathematical direction

Define a reopening kernel over admissible continuations and a settlement functional (F(C,A,H,\varepsilon)). Prove sufficient conditions under which confidence amplification yields a bounded reopening probability without implying expanded capability. Then characterize when two construction branches induce equivalent finality indices and when disagreement forces a fork.

## Suggested falsification test

Show that every apparent finality diagnosis either changes under equally admissible adversary models or reduces to ordinary dynamics plus a stopping threshold. If the typed account adds no invariant discrimination or prediction, the reductive rival should be preferred for these cases.

## Relationship classification

**Analogy.** Avalanche supplies a precise model of conflict, evidence sampling, metastability, and scoped settlement. Those relations illuminate the candidate, but probabilistic network consensus is neither equivalent to nor established as the mechanism of physical finality.
