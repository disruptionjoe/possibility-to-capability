## Steelman thesis

Possibility to Capability is really an attempt to extract representation-independent diagnostic structure from physical descriptions: it refuses to confuse a change of presentation, visibility, or chosen control realization with a genuine enlargement of what can be done. Its six terms are best read as candidate invariants and obstructions in a partial diagram, not as stages of reality.

## Summary of understanding

In representation-theoretic language, a physical construction supplies a space of admissible objects, an action of a group, semigroup, or Lie algebra encoding dynamics, stable covariants or substructures that can serve as records, and selected observable/control subalgebras governing access. Capability concerns the resulting task-orbit or reachability structure only after representation, resources, access, and control are transported through a declared common normalization. Candidate finality is then a bounded obstruction to factoring a settlement through the earlier structures or lifting it to an admissible continuation. Construction forks correspond to genuinely different categories, actions, or morphism classes and therefore must not be averaged away. On this reading, the repository is an invariance-and-obstruction program with strict provenance, not an ontology.

## Strongest insight

The access/capability distinction is the sharpest idea. Restricting an observable algebra can hide components of a representation; enlarging it can reveal them without changing the representation or its dynamical orbit. Likewise, a basis change can expose a decomposition without creating new structure. Requiring normalized task-set change is an attempt to distinguish disclosure from genuine enlargement. Representation theory makes the implied standard precise: a capability verdict deserves physical standing only if it is preserved by the relevant intertwiners.

## Strongest criticism

The repository has not yet defined the groupoid of admissible representations or the functorial transport of normalization data. Pure label reversal is far weaker than equivalence under gauge choice, duality, polarization, coarse-graining, or change of generators. Until those maps are fixed by source-owned physical equivalence judgments, the chosen representation and access/control subalgebras may manufacture the capability verdict. The danger is worse for noncompact, infinite-dimensional, or non-semisimple actions, where decomposition need not be canonical.

## Hidden assumptions

- Physically equivalent constructions form a tractable class with explicit intertwiners.
- Records, access, tasks, and controls transport coherently under those intertwiners.
- The normalization frame does not already encode the desired diagnosis.
- Factorization is searched in a sufficiently rich, preregistered category of morphisms and extensions.
- Diagnostic behavior composes sensibly under sums, products, coarse-graining, and subsystem restriction.
- Any proposed obstruction is stable under admissible enlargement rather than an artifact of a small representation category.

## Rose

The machinery is unusually honest about nulls, blocks, incomparable branches, and `HIERARCHY_REVISION`. That posture matches mature representation theory, where inequivalent representations and failed decompositions are mathematical information rather than inconvenient exceptions. The GU-001 provenance stop also correctly establishes no representation-theoretic or physical conclusion.

## Bud

The most promising development is to replace “representation-respecting” with a checkable naturality contract. A real packet with two source-certified equivalent presentations could turn the present vocabulary into a genuine invariant test rather than a disciplined taxonomy.

## Thorn

The most dangerous failure is presentation dependence disguised as physics: capability or finality changes when one changes an equally admissible realization, decomposition, observable algebra, or control algebra. Repeated forks of that kind would favor operational-context primacy or explanatory plurality.

## Confidence

8/10 that this is the strongest interpretation of what the repository is about. This rates the explanatory framing, not the truth of the physics. With zero accepted real packets and zero completed real gate runs, confidence in physical invariance is not yet earned.

## Suggested experiment

Preregister one real case in two source-certified equivalent representations and one intentionally inequivalent construction. Freeze the transformation/intertwiner, dynamics generators, record subspaces, observable and control algebras, resource budget, task vocabulary, normalization, and continuation class before classification. Transport every datum along the equivalence and compare the full diagnosis branchwise. Agreement across the equivalent pair and principled disagreement only at the inequivalent branch would favor the candidate; any provenance failure remains terminal rather than evidence.

## Suggested theorem or mathematical direction

Define a groupoid of physical presentations and attach to each object a diagram consisting of an admissible space, dynamical action, record covariants, access/control subalgebras, and normalized task relation. Prove an equivariance theorem: any source-certified intertwining equivalence that preserves the declared resources and admissible continuations induces an isomorphism of diagnostic diagrams and hence the same diagnosis. Candidate finality could then be explored as a relative obstruction class—perhaps in a quotient category, extension group, or cohomology—always scoped to the declared category rather than promoted to absolute non-factorability.

## Suggested falsification test

Find two source-certified physically equivalent presentations connected by an exact intertwiner, transport all frozen witnesses and normalization data, and obtain different diagnoses. If independent reviewers cannot identify omitted non-invariant structure, the representation-independent candidate fails and requires `HIERARCHY_REVISION`. A constraint-first model that functorially reproduces every distinction with fewer assumptions would also defeat the need for the six-type assembly.

## Relationship classification

`analogy`. The candidate strongly resembles representation theory's passage from presentations to invariants, restrictions, orbits, intertwiners, decompositions, and obstructions. But no functor, equivalence class, or invariance theorem currently identifies the two structures, so `homology`, `reduction`, and `equivalence` would overstate the evidence. The analogy could mature into a structural homology only after substantive representation-change tests on accepted real packets.
