## Steelman thesis

Possibility to Capability is an audit language for identifying *what changed in an explanatory model*: admissible model class, transition mechanism, persistent traces, observation/control interface, attainable interventions, or—only after bounded adversarial search—a settlement not reopened by retained causal alternatives. It aims to prevent observational information, controllability, and genuine enlargement of intervention-relative attainability from being conflated.

## Summary of understanding

From causal inference, the six diagnoses have a natural but provisional translation. Possibility is the admissible family of causal models or supports; dynamics is the structural/transition mechanism; records are persistent variables carrying distinctions forward; access is the observation and control channel available to a policy; capability is the set of task outcomes or interventional distributions attainable under a declared common observation, intervention, and resource frame; candidate finality is invariance of a settlement against a preregistered class of factorizations, interventions, and continuations.

The arrow is not a causal DAG but a typed checklist for claims about causal change. Current support is deterministic synthetic discrimination plus disciplined blocked intake. With zero accepted real packets and zero completed real gate runs, this is an instrument, not a physical hierarchy. GU-001 supplies no receiver-side causal evidence because provenance blocked every substantive gate.

## Strongest insight

The access/capability split is a causal-category safeguard. A better sensor can change conditioning and policy performance without changing the underlying attainable set under a common information frame; a persistent record can predict later events without being readable or manipulable; transfer entropy can be positive without identifying an intervention effect. Requiring normalized task-set comparison forces the question that causal inference asks through `do`-operators: did the mechanism-supported intervention envelope change, or did we merely learn to condition on it?

## Strongest criticism

The normalization frame may do all the argumentative work. “Hold description, representation, resources, access, and control fixed” is not automatically a well-defined causal operation: these factors can be constitutive, entangled, or cross-world incompatible. Without modular interventions and an invariant estimand, the boundary becomes reviewer-selected. No finite causal-discovery or factorization search establishes absolute finality; latent confounding, Markov equivalence, unmeasured interventions, and enlarged state spaces limit it to a declared model and intervention class.

## Hidden assumptions

- The chosen variables and system boundary support stable causal mechanisms.
- Relevant interventions are well-defined, modular, and sufficiently supported; consistency and positivity are not badly violated.
- Normalization does not condition on a collider, erase a mediator, or compare incompatible task semantics.
- Source-certified representation changes preserve the interventional content relevant to diagnosis.
- Records, observation channels, controls, and resources can sometimes be varied or held fixed independently.
- The admissible continuation and factorization classes are broad enough to give rivals a serious test.

## Rose

The machinery preserves causal non-identifiability rather than converting it into a positive story. `BLOCKED`, construction forks, incomparability, and hierarchy revision are appropriate when observational evidence cannot orient an edge or interventions do not transport.

## Bud

Formalizing capability as a representation-invariant set of attainable interventional distributions could bridge causal inference, control, and information theory. The key development is a quotient separating changes in observation sigma-algebras from changes in mechanisms and attainable intervention outcomes.

## Thorn

The vocabulary could redescribe every causal model after the fact while adding no identifiable estimand or prediction. “Finality” is especially vulnerable to becoming residual ignorance with a prestigious label if the model class or continuation set is chosen too narrowly.

## Confidence

8/10 that this is the strongest causal-inference interpretation. It fits the non-causal typing and intervention-centered access/capability distinction; no real packet has tested whether the required normalization exists.

## Suggested experiment

Use a real, source-issued packet from a partially observed control system with three preregistered interventions: create a durable but controller-hidden trace; improve sensing only; and alter the physical mechanism so a new target becomes reachable with the same sensors, actuators, resources, and task vocabulary. Blind reviewers should classify record, access, and capability before seeing labels. Compare transfer entropy, predictive accuracy, estimated causal effects, and the reachable interventional outcome set. The candidate earns support only if those measures separate in the predicted ways across independently defended normalization frames.

## Suggested theorem or mathematical direction

Define an object `(M, O, I, R, T)`: causal model, observation map, admissible intervention family, resources, and task semantics. Characterize necessary and sufficient conditions under which two such objects differ only by access versus by normalized capability. Then prove invariance, or exhibit an impossibility theorem, under interventional equivalence, causal-variable refinement, and source-certified representation maps. Construction-dependent failures should remain indexed rather than averaged.

## Suggested falsification test

Preregister two source-certified representations with identical interventional distributions but different graphical factorizations. If they receive different diagnoses, the typing is representation-dependent. More broadly, if repeated real cases admit no invariant access/capability quotient, or if one expanded causal model reproduces every diagnostic distinction and reopening behavior with fewer assumptions, favor operational-context or reductive dynamics and revise the hierarchy.

## Relationship classification

`homology`. There is a substantive structural correspondence—model family, mechanism, persistent mediator, observation/control channel, intervention envelope, absorbing or invariant class—but not an equivalence or reduction. Causal inference can formalize and test much of the candidate, while finality and source-sovereignty machinery extend beyond standard causal semantics.
