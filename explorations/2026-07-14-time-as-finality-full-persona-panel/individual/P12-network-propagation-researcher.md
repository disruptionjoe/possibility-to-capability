## Steelman thesis

Possibility to Capability is really an identifiability discipline for propagation systems: it asks whether an observed change belongs to the support of admissible network states, the transition operator, persistent memory, observation/control channels, the normalized reachable task set, or a settlement not reopened by any admissible continuation. Its wager is not that systems traverse these in order, but that keeping these graph-theoretic objects distinct prevents diffusion, synchronization, epidemics, and information flow from being explained by the wrong kind of change.

## Summary of understanding

In network terms, possibility is the allowed graph/state/history family; dynamics is the update, contagion, transport, or coupling rule within an assessed family; records are persistent state distinctions; access is the set of paths or channels through which distinctions can be observed, conditioned on, or controlled; capability is a strict change in normalized operational reachability after representation, resources, access, and control are held fixed; and finality is only a bounded candidate when a settlement survives preregistered reduction and reopening attacks. These diagnoses may coexist, be skipped, become incomparable, fork by temporal/multilayer/coarse-grained construction, or force `HIERARCHY_REVISION`.

The current evidence establishes disciplined machinery and synthetic discriminability, not a physical propagation law. Thirty-five stipulated cases test the classifier. No real packet has been accepted, no real eight-gate run completed, and GU-001 is motivation only: provenance is blocked and every substantive gate is unrun.

## Strongest insight

The access-capability separation is the deepest contribution. In contagion or control, adding a sensor, communication edge, seed-placement privilege, or query channel can reveal or activate outcomes already supported by the system without enlarging its normalized intrinsic task set. Conversely, a changed coupling or transition kernel may enlarge controllability even with the interface fixed. Requiring that distinction blocks the common inference from “more reached” to “more capable.”

## Strongest criticism

In network science, access is often constitutive of capability. Controllability depends on actuator placement; epidemic intervention depends on surveillance; information flow depends on channel topology. Holding access and control fixed may therefore remove the very relational structure that makes the task meaningful. The normalization frame can quietly decide the verdict, especially when temporal, higher-order, multilayer, and coarse-grained representations induce different reachable sets.

## Hidden assumptions

The program assumes that cases admit a stable node/state/task vocabulary; that relevant history can be represented without losing non-Markovian effects; that source-certified representation equivalences can be obtained; that records can be separated from downstream readability; and that factorization and continuation classes are broad enough to challenge apparent closure. It also assumes a one-chain evaluation can represent genuine distributed support without manufacturing or discarding success.

## Rose

Failure preservation is a real strength. Blocking, nulls, incomparable outcomes, construction forks, and hierarchy revision are treated as results rather than noise. This is unusually appropriate for network models, where aggregation choices routinely create false thresholds, false synchronization, and false causal paths.

## Bud

The most promising development is a representation-respecting theory of normalized reachability across temporal, multilayer, and coarse-grained networks. If equivalent constructions preserve the diagnosis while genuinely different constructions retain explicit forks, the framework could become a useful cross-domain interface without becoming an ontology.

## Thorn

The dangerous failure mode is elegant taxonomic overfitting: increasingly refined interfaces classify stipulated examples while every real network case remains blocked, construction-relative, or reducible to ordinary dynamics and boundary conditions. “Finality” could then become a prestigious name for finite observation windows, metastability, absorbing approximations, or missing edges.

## Confidence

8/10 that this steelman best captures what the repository is about. This rates interpretive fit, not the truth, completeness, novelty, or universality of the physics.

## Suggested experiment

Preregister a source-issued experiment on an instrumented distributed-consensus or spreading network. Pair (A) a change only to observation/control channels with fixed coupling and resources, and (B) a change to coupling or transition rules while restoring the same channels and resources. Freeze task vocabulary, rival normalization frames, temporal and aggregated constructions, and predictions before classification. Measure reachable targets, recovery after perturbation, synchronization error, and information-spread profiles. Preserve disagreement among constructions; do not average it into a pass.

## Suggested theorem or mathematical direction

Define the diagnoses over a networked transition system with observation and intervention morphisms. Seek a commuting-diagram theorem: under source-certified bisimulation or lumpable graph quotient, possibility, record, access, and normalized capability diagnoses are invariant exactly when the quotient preserves admissible histories, persistent distinctions, observation/control morphisms, and task reachability. Characterize failures as construction forks or hierarchy revision. A complementary result should give necessary and sufficient conditions for an access intervention to leave normalized capability unchanged.

## Suggested falsification test

Take source-certified equivalent temporal, multilayer, event-graph, and coarse-grained representations of the same frozen cases. Blindly classify each while a reductive challenger predicts from the full transition model and boundary conditions alone. Falsify or substantially revise the candidate if equivalent representations change diagnoses, if no non-circular normalization separates access from capability, or if the reductive model matches every discrimination and intervention prediction with fewer assumptions.

## Relationship classification

`homology`. The correspondence is structural, not merely verbal: admissible support, transition kernels, persistent state, observation/control channels, reachable task sets, and closed or non-reopenable classes are recurring network objects that map onto the six diagnoses. It is not equivalence or reduction, because the mapping is construction-dependent and no real packet has shown that the repository's full gate structure is preserved in an empirical network case. This is an interpretive homology, not evidence for the candidate.
