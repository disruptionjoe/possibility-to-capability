# Twenty personas read the bounded fiber theorem from outside

Lightweight inline panel (house rule: personas inline, one worker). Input: the bounded-fiber-theorem
story — a shared consistency layer that provably transports the sign bit without ever determining it;
orientation requires a flip-odd primitive (= the posit); arrow ⊥ sector; posit's physics name = the
sector-asymmetric spectral condition; per-block burden under partitioned transport. Each persona:
the story in their idiom + what their discipline sees that we aren't seeing. Exploration tier; these are
leads, not results.

1. **MMO network architect.** You built a flawless replication layer and found the one field with no
   authoritative source. We'd never *derive* that field — we'd designate a **write authority**. Unseen: the
   question isn't derivation, it's **authority topology** — who (what physical subsystem) holds the write
   lock on the bit, and what does the lock's location predict?
2. **Distributed-consensus theorist.** This is an FLP-shaped impossibility: sign-agreement needs an oracle.
   Unseen: impossibility results spawn a **weakest-oracle program** (like Ω for consensus). Door 2 should be
   sharpened to: *what is the WEAKEST flip-odd primitive sufficient?* — a minimality theorem, not a menu.
3. **Time-series analyst.** The sign is **absolute phase**: unrecoverable from any stationary statistics of
   the process itself; only cross-correlation against an **external reference signal** recovers it. Unseen:
   stop deriving, start hunting the reference — which physical observable is phase-locked to the sector?
4. **Graph-database engineer.** Query languages are deliberately isomorphism-invariant — they *cannot*
   distinguish isomorphic instances; that's what **surrogate keys** are for. Unseen: the bit may be an
   **identity datum, not a structural one**. Databases stopped trying to derive identity from structure
   decades ago; the theorem is the physics version of that lesson.
5. **Hypergraph theorist.** Pairwise balance is exhausted, and the binary-alphabet lemma is tight — but
   **|V| ≥ 3 with arity ≥ 3 jointly** is where genuinely new invariants live: **cyclic orientations** are
   invariant under even relabelings and odd under transpositions, so "label-free" fractures into a
   hierarchy. Unseen: door 1 has a concrete mathematical address (cyclic ℤ/3 structure), not just a caveat.
6. **Compiler engineer.** The two orbit members are two **ABIs**: every program compiles identically under
   either; the convention only gets fixed at the **link step**, when separately compiled modules must
   interoperate. Unseen: find the physics link step — the first interaction where two independently
   specified systems must agree on the convention. That's where fixing happens without derivation.
7. **Double-entry accountant.** Debit vs credit is underivable from ledger structure; it's fixed by **whose
   book it is**. Unseen: the bit may be **perspectival** — check whether the two orbit members are the
   matter/antimatter-style perspective swap, in which case the question dissolves rather than resolves.
8. **Evolutionary biologist.** This is **homochirality**: life uses L-amino acids, nobody derives it —
   frozen accident amplified by autocatalysis, possibly seeded by **weak-force parity violation**. Unseen,
   and important: **nature already contains a flip-odd primitive — the electroweak sector violates parity.**
   Door 4 has a known street address, and it is literally *chirality* — the very thing GU's generation
   question is about.
9. **Cryptographer.** The bit is a shared secret and all channels are public and symmetric: Shannon says no
   protocol establishes it without pre-shared material (the posit) or an **asymmetry assumption** (a
   trapdoor). Unseen: look for the trapdoor — a physically hard-to-reverse process — rather than a proof.
10. **Control theorist.** The sign is an **unobservable mode**. Engineering doesn't derive unobservable
    modes; it either proves you never need them or **adds a sensor**. Unseen: formulate the minimal
    measurement that would make the bit observable — the "add a sensor" spec is a concrete deliverable.
11. **Statistical physicist.** This is **spontaneous symmetry breaking** done properly: nobody derives the
    direction of magnetization; you derive the *instability* (that a sign must be chosen) and the
    *susceptibility* (how fluctuations amplify). Unseen: "derive the sign" may be a malformed question —
    the well-formed ones are instability + amplification mechanism, and we've only done the first.
12. **Linguist.** Saussure: the sign is arbitrary; meaning lives in differences; yet languages work because
    convention is **transmitted**. Unseen: study the **transmission operator** — how records inherit the
    convention from parent records — rather than the static structure. Lineage, not law.
13. **Cartographer.** Map handedness can't be derived from terrain; it's fixed by standards bodies — i.e.
    by the **collective of users**, not the territory. Unseen: the fixing institution is the observer
    collective itself; ask what the minimal "standards body" is in the physics.
14. **CRDT/replication specialist.** Every production system resolves exactly this with an
    **identity-based tiebreaker** (node ID in last-writer-wins), and pays for it without ceremony. Unseen:
    the posit costs **one bit, once** — theory says you must pay it, engineering says it's cheap. Maybe
    physics paid it the same way and the interesting question is only *when*.
15. **Paleomagnetist.** Earth's field flips; rocks record which orientation was realized. Records don't
    *derive* the sign — **remembering the sign is what records are for.** Unseen, and inverting: the fiber
    is untransported by structure precisely because it is the *content* records carry, not the *form*. The
    program may have proved "content ≠ form" in its own vocabulary.
16. **Music theorist.** All harmonic structure is transposition-invariant (relative intervals); absolute
    pitch (A=440) is fixed by a physical artifact — the tuning fork. Unseen: the reference is always an
    **artifact**, not a rule. What is the tuning fork of the vacuum?
17. **Immunologist.** Self/nonself isn't derived; it's **learned once, developmentally** (thymic negative
    selection), then enforced for life. Unseen: look for a **developmental epoch** where the bit was set
    and frozen — cosmologically: the electroweak symmetry-breaking era — rather than a timeless principle.
18. **Game theorist / legal scholar.** Driving-side conventions: pure coordination equilibria, selected by
    **salience and history** (Schelling points), self-enforcing afterward. Unseen: the mature theory here
    is **equilibrium selection**, not equilibrium existence — import it; "how does a convention lock in"
    is a solved genre.
19. **Deep-learning researcher.** Networks carry exact sign-flip weight symmetries; independently trained
    models each break them arbitrarily; **model alignment (git re-basin) can always discover the RELATIVE
    sign between two models and never the absolute one.** Unseen: strong evidence that only relative signs
    are operationally meaningful — and alignment algorithms are constructive procedures for exactly the
    relative structure the theorem says is derivable.
20. **Historian of science.** Underivable global data have two respectable historical endings: they get
    **relativized away** (absolute velocity → relativity) or they become **measured constants** (CP-violation
    phases). Unseen: prepare both exits *now* — the program should know in advance which result it would
    accept as "the bit is unphysical" vs "the bit is a constant of nature."

## Chairman synthesis — the convergent signals we weren't seeing

1. **The relational-dissolution reading (7, 12, 15, 19, 20 — five independent arrivals).** Only *relative*
   signs may be operationally meaningful; the absolute member may be gauge. Concrete new question for the
   program: **is there any observable that depends on the absolute orbit member rather than on relative
   alignment?** If provably no — the bit is unphysical and the question dissolves (historian's exit (a)).
   This is a *different* closure than deriving the value, and nothing in the three swings addresses it.
2. **Nature's flip-odd primitive already exists (8, 17).** Electroweak **parity violation** is a physical,
   flip-odd, epoch-set datum — and it is chirality, the very subject of GU's generation question. Door 4
   is not hypothetical; the physics literature on Krein-sector/chirality coupling is the address. (Also
   consistent with GU's own founding framing: the count "enters as chirality does.")
3. **The weakest-oracle program (2, 9, 10).** Convert door 2 from a menu into a minimality question: the
   weakest flip-odd primitive sufficient, the minimal sensor, the minimal trapdoor. That is a theorem-shaped
   target the current no-go machinery could actually attack.
4. **The SSB reframe (11, 18).** Derive the *instability and amplification*, not the direction. The program
   has proved the instability half (a sign must be chosen; structure can't choose it); the susceptibility /
   lock-in-dynamics half (equilibrium selection, transmission operator — 12) is untouched and well-posed.
5. **A concrete door-1 sharpening (5).** Cyclic orientations under |V| ≥ 3: "label-free" fractures into
   even-relabeling-invariant classes. A finite fixture can test whether cyclic-orientation invariants are
   flip-odd yet even-label-free — the one mathematically new escape the panel produced.
