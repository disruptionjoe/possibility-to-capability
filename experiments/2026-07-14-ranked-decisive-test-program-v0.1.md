# Ranked Decisive-Test Program v0.1

## Purpose

This program ranks the next tests by expected ability to discriminate the
typed-partial-diagnostic candidate from reductive dynamics, operational-context
relativism, constraint-first structure, and explanatory plurality.

It is a proposal, not cross-repository authorization. Each source owner retains
its truth, packet issuance, constructions, grades, and status. JoeOps may
sequence work but does not own the research verdict. Possibility to Capability
owns only the neutral receiver contracts, preregistered comparisons, and local
results produced from admissible frozen packets.

## Update vocabulary

Possible updates are qualitative because current repo evidence does not support
a numerical prior:

- `FAVORS_CANDIDATE`: the candidate makes and survives a discrimination a
  rival does not match under equal assumptions.
- `FAVORS_RIVAL`: a rival explains the observation with equal or greater
  discrimination and fewer unsupported commitments.
- `REVISE_HIERARCHY`: the evidence exposes a stable missing, collapsed, or
  construction-dependent type.
- `REMAINS_UNDERDETERMINED`: the test is admissible but does not discriminate.
- `BLOCKED`: a prerequisite is absent; downstream judgments are not run.

These are receiver updates, not source claim promotions.

## Rank 1: Complete one real source-issued intake and preregistered gate run

### Decisive question

Can any real source-issued packet survive packet validation, non-upgrading
receiver assessment, and the complete gate chain while preserving source
meaning and one construction/argument chain?

GU-001 remains the founding candidate. If GU issues a valid v0.2 bundle, use
it. This test must not silently substitute a different packet for completion of
the founding sequence. A second source packet can be prepared as an additional
case, but it receives its own identifier and cannot make GU-001 pass.

### Why ranked first

Every current positive result is synthetic and the one real intake is blocked.
One admissible real end-to-end case changes the evidential class of the whole
program more than another schema or larger synthetic suite.

### Owners and boundaries

- GU owns GU-001 issuance, exact source wording, artifacts, grades,
  premise/dependency graph, status, residuals, and bundle digests.
- TI and TaF own any source-side boundary packets or claims required by the
  case; their partial results cannot be copied into GU-001.
- Possibility to Capability owns intake validation, the separate receiving
  assessment, gate judgments, transition diagnosis, and local result.
- JoeOps may sequence requests. It owns no scientific pass.
- No receiver may create a source-issued identifier, digest, convergence
  result, or independence type.

### Prerequisites

1. A newly pinned source revision containing a source-issued candidate bundle.
2. All raw blobs and the canonical `ptc-frozen-bundle-v1` manifest.
3. Matching source-issued manifest, packet, and bundle digests.
4. Exact source premise/dependency structure and joint argument.
5. A preregistration of receiver constructions, normalization frame,
   factorization search, rival forecasts, and stop conditions before gate
   outcomes are inspected.

### Possible outcomes and updates

- **Blocked at provenance:** `BLOCKED`; supports process integrity but leaves
  all physical stories underdetermined.
- **Valid packet, formation or construction fails:** favors plurality or a
  source-specific account; revise the interface if the failure is expressive
  rather than evidential.
- **Passes through capability but finality is blocked/indeterminate:** supports
  the typed discrimination while leaving finality open; does not favor an
  irreducible-finality story.
- **Candidate finality factors through an earlier type:** favors reductive or
  constraint-first rivals and weakens the candidate's need for finality.
- **One-chain, neutral, independently reviewed full pass:** favors the
  candidate over claims that the interface cannot form a real joint object,
  but still establishes only that case and construction.
- **Construction branches disagree:** `REVISE_HIERARCHY` or favor operational
  context; never average the branches into success.

### Feasibility and stop

Technically ready on the receiver side; evidence-blocked upstream. Stop at
provenance until a source-issued candidate exists. The current GU preflight is
historical evidence and is never rewritten into a pass.

## Rank 2: Access-versus-capability paired intervention test

### Decisive question

Can independently specified cases distinguish an access-only intervention from
a normalized capability change without the normalization frame entailing the
answer?

### Why ranked second

The access-capability boundary is the load-bearing novel discrimination in the
plain-English story and the strongest point of attack for operational-context
relativism. It can be tested before any claim about finality.

### Construction

Preregister at least two paired real cases over a shared task vocabulary:

1. an intervention intended to change observability, reachability, or control
   while normalized task capability remains equal; and
2. an intervention intended to change the normalized task set after the same
   declared description, representation, resource, access, and control factors
   are held fixed.

Source evidence issuers provide frozen packets without seeing the receiver's
diagnostic labels. Independent reviewers propose the normalization frame and a
context-constitutive alternative before outcomes are classified.

### Owners and boundaries

- Each physical or formal source owns its interventions, measurements,
  evidence, and claim status.
- Possibility to Capability owns the comparison contract and classification.
- An operational-context reviewer owns no source truth but supplies a frozen
  rival construction.
- JoeOps selects and sequences cases; it does not decide the diagnosis.

### Prerequisites

- A source-issued frozen packet for each case.
- A common task vocabulary or an explicit proof that no common vocabulary is
  admissible.
- At least two independently defended normalization frames, including the
  strongest access-constitutive construction.
- Preregistered intervention predictions that differ between `ACCESS_CHANGE`
  and capability enlargement/restriction.

### Possible outcomes and updates

- **Stable access-only and capability diagnoses with distinct predictions:**
  `FAVORS_CANDIDATE` over operational-context collapse.
- **All admissible frames agree only after access is made constitutive:**
  `FAVORS_RIVAL` and revise the normalization rule.
- **Equally admissible frames disagree with no invariant quotient:** favors
  operational-context relativism or `CONSTRUCTION_FORK`.
- **No shared task comparison is meaningful:** favors plurality and may require
  a new type rather than a forced verdict.
- **Pairs are empirically indistinguishable:** `REMAINS_UNDERDETERMINED` and
  weakens claims that the boundary has explanatory value.

### Feasibility and stop

Feasible if a source can issue paired intervention data and task semantics.
Stop if the normalization is chosen after seeing the desired answer or if
source and receiver evidence are blended.

## Rank 3: Adversarial finality-factorization tournament

### Decisive question

Does any proposed settlement retain explanatory residue after the strongest
preregistered attempts to factor it through enlarged possibility spaces,
dynamics, boundary conditions, records, access, normalized capability, and
constraint structure?

### Why ranked third

Finality is the candidate story's highest-risk term. Testing it before access
and capability are stable would be circular; testing it after them directly
distinguishes the candidate from reductive dynamics and constraint-first
stories.

### Construction

For each source-issued finality candidate, preregister independent factorization
teams or arguments:

- strongest enlarged-dynamics/coarse-graining model;
- strongest boundary-condition or global-constraint model;
- strongest records/access/capability factorization; and
- strongest admissible-continuation reopening construction.

The finality evaluator is blinded to preferred branch labels. `NO_FACTOR_FOUND`
is always scoped to the declared search; it never becomes absolute proof.

### Owners and boundaries

- TaF or another proposing source owns its finality candidate, evidence, and
  claim status.
- GU, TI, or other contributors own only their source packets and arguments.
- Possibility to Capability owns the factorization tournament contract and
  receiver result.
- No missing factorization leg may be borrowed from a different construction
  to complete a pass.

### Prerequisites

- Rank 1 has produced at least one accepted real packet and a working source-
  receiver interface.
- Rank 2 has either stabilized access/capability or retained its construction
  fork explicitly.
- A named settlement witness, factorization search space, admissible
  continuation class, and rival forecasts are frozen before evaluation.

### Possible outcomes and updates

- **A defensible factor is found:** strongly favors the corresponding reductive
  or constraint-first rival; finality gate fails for that case.
- **No factor is found but search coverage is weak:**
  `REMAINS_UNDERDETERMINED`; finality remains a candidate only.
- **No factor is found under broad, independent, preregistered searches and no
  admissible continuation reopens the case:** favors the candidate need for a
  distinct finality diagnosis, still scoped to the case and search.
- **Different admissible constructions alternate between factorable and
  non-factorable:** favors context or plurality and requires construction-
  indexed results.
- **The finality criterion assumes the settlement it purports to establish:**
  invalidate the test and reopen the contract; do not count it against or for
  any physical story.

### Feasibility and stop

Methodologically feasible but scientifically demanding. Stop any claimed pass
if the factorization search is post hoc, the continuation class excludes an
unwanted reopening without reason, or irreversibility is used as settlement.

## Rank 4: Representation and construction-fork invariance challenge

### Decisive question

Does the same frozen physical content receive the same diagnosis under
substantively equivalent representations and across all materially admissible
construction branches?

### Why ranked fourth

Pure label invariance is already tested synthetically. The larger story needs a
harder result: stability under transformations that change formalism without
changing the physical case, while retaining genuinely different constructions.

### Owners and boundaries

- Source owners certify or contest physical equivalence of representations and
  issue each construction branch.
- Possibility to Capability preregisters the invariants and classifies each
  branch separately.
- No receiver may declare two source constructions equivalent merely because
  that makes the diagnoses agree.

### Prerequisites

- At least one accepted real packet with two equivalent representations or two
  admissible constructions.
- Frozen transformation maps, retained branch identifiers, and predicted
  invariant and variant fields.
- Independent review of whether the transformation preserves the physical
  content relevant to the diagnosis.

### Possible outcomes and updates

- **Equivalent representations preserve diagnosis:** favors the candidate's
  representation-respecting ambition.
- **Diagnosis changes under an equivalent representation:** strong
  `REVISE_HIERARCHY`; the current type is representation-dependent.
- **Different constructions yield different diagnoses for principled reasons:**
  retain `CONSTRUCTION_FORK`; favors operational context or plurality rather
  than declaring failure away.
- **One deeper constraint object collapses all forks and reproduces every
  distinction:** favors the constraint-first rival.
- **Equivalence itself is contested:** `REMAINS_UNDERDETERMINED`; the receiver
  cannot settle source equivalence by fiat.

### Feasibility and stop

Feasible after real packet intake. Stop if branch restoration changes the
result and the report tries to publish only the favorable branch.

## Rank 5: Cross-domain hierarchy-breaker test

### Decisive question

Does the typed diagnostic retain meaning on a difficult non-founding case, or
does a second domain expose a stable missing type or the impossibility of one
joint explanatory grammar?

### Why ranked fifth

This is the strongest universality and plurality test, but it should follow one
physics-native end-to-end case so that interface failure is not confused with
an untested setup. WI-069 is a particularly valuable sibling source because its
obstruction/transduction object is related but intentionally not subsumed.

### Owners and boundaries

- WI-069 or another selected source owns its obstruction, transduction,
  measurement, agency, and claim truth.
- Possibility to Capability owns only the frozen classification and any local
  hierarchy-revision result.
- The two repos exchange typed frozen packets. Neither inherits the other's
  conclusions.
- JoeOps sequences the program; it does not merge the siblings.

### Prerequisites

- At least one completed real physics-native gate run.
- A source-issued packet selected for difficulty rather than expected fit.
- Preregistered mapping rules, prohibited source-specific redefinitions, and
  a first-class `HIERARCHY_REVISION` outcome.

### Possible outcomes and updates

- **Same types and gates discriminate without source-specific rewriting:**
  favors the candidate as a general interface, not yet a universal ontology.
- **A stable missing type is required:** `REVISE_HIERARCHY`; repeated cases
  favor plurality.
- **Mapping erases source load-bearing distinctions:** favors plurality and
  rejects the attempted import.
- **The sibling's obstruction/transduction account predicts distinctions the
  hierarchy misses:** favors the sibling-specific or pluralist story.
- **Both frameworks preserve distinct truth and yield reciprocal new tests:**
  favors typed interoperability while leaving subsumption false.

### Feasibility and stop

Feasible only through frozen bidirectional interfaces. Stop if the receiver
renames source concepts until they fit, absorbs WI-069 truth, or treats analogy
as a pass.

## Sequence and fan-out posture

The ranks are logically sequenced by evidential dependency, but preparation
can fan out without merging results:

- While Rank 1 awaits source issuance, separate teams can preregister Rank 2
  normalization rivals and Rank 3 factorization families as proposals.
- Source packet preparation, receiver validator review, and rival forecast
  drafting may proceed in parallel only when each has a distinct owner and
  writable repository.
- Actual receiver verdicts remain serialized: validate packet, assess without
  upgrade, run gates, diagnose transition, then update rivals.
- No parallel partial pass can satisfy a later rank or make GU-001 importable.

## Program-level falsifier

If several well-chosen real packets either cannot cross the source boundary
without semantic distortion or repeatedly return construction-dependent,
source-specific, or hierarchy-revision outcomes with no stable invariant, the
right conclusion is not to add enough qualifiers to save the arc. It is to
prefer a pluralist or alternative account and preserve the failed synthesis as
the result.
