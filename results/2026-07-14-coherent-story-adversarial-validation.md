# Coherent Story v0.1: Adversarial Validation and Premortem

## Result

The candidate synthesis passes a structural overclaim audit as a **provisional
typed partial diagnostic**. It does not pass as a universal physical story, an
ontology, a chronology, a confirmation of GU, or evidence for irreducible
finality.

The strongest defensible statement remains:

> The six terms form a coherent, falsifiable diagnostic vocabulary in the
> implemented synthetic construction. Real-case discrimination among this
> candidate and reductive, operational-context, constraint-first, and pluralist
> rivals has not yet occurred.

## Audit construction

The audit checks the structured ledger and synthesis against six named failure
families:

1. overclaiming;
2. circular finality;
3. access-capability collapse;
4. source leakage;
5. artificial success; and
6. construction dependence.

The deterministic validator also rejects chronology smuggling, weak rivals,
and references that leak absolute local paths. Nine mutation controls each
change one protected property and must produce a targeted error.

This is a semantic-boundary audit. It does not validate the truth of physical
evidence, establish novelty, or replace expert review.

## Findings

### 1. Overclaiming

**Attack:** turn a useful vocabulary into a proved universal explanation, or
turn familiar ingredients into a novelty claim.

**Controls:**

- the central hypothesis is grammatically and semantically a question;
- verdict is `OPEN` and promotion is `NOT_PROMOTED`;
- evidence ceiling is `synthetic_plus_blocked_real_intake`;
- conceptual ingredients explicitly carry `novelty_claimed: false`;
- synthetic results carry `physical_claim: false`;
- universality and completeness remain `OPEN_UNSUPPORTED`; and
- no numerical likelihood is assigned.

**Current finding:** pass at structural grade. The synthesis separates familiar
ingredients, repo-owned assembly, synthetic results, blocked motivation, and
open claims.

**Residual risk:** language such as "larger picture" can invite readers to
promote a diagnostic vocabulary into an ontology. A literature and priority
audit is still required before any external novelty statement.

### 2. Circular finality

**Attack:** define finality as whatever cannot be explained, interpret failure
to find a factor as proof that no factor exists, or use irreversibility as
settlement.

**Controls:**

- the only admissible label is `FINALITY_CANDIDATE_ONLY`;
- settlement, a named factorization-search scope, no factor found within that
  scope, and no reopening by admissible continuation are all required;
- bounded non-factorization explicitly does not establish absolute
  non-reducibility;
- `irreversibility_sufficient` is false; and
- the decisive test program gives reductive dynamics, boundary conditions,
  constraint structure, preceding diagnostic types, and continuation their
  strongest preregistered factorization attempts.

**Current finding:** pass at structural grade. No real finality gate has run;
physical irreducibility remains not assessable.

**Residual risk:** the declared factorization search and admissible-continuation
class can be chosen to exclude the strongest rival. Independent preregistration
and review are load-bearing.

### 3. Access-capability collapse

**Attack:** call newly reachable or executable tasks new intrinsic capability,
or choose a normalization frame that already assumes the conclusion.

**Controls:**

- access is defined by conditioning, observability, reachability, or control;
- capability requires a strict normalized task-set change;
- the frame names description, representation, resources, access, and control
  as comparison factors;
- raw task availability is not capability;
- the access-constitutive construction remains live; and
- Rank 2 preregisters paired interventions and rival normalization frames.

**Current finding:** pass in the synthetic construction only. The implemented
cases show that the interface can keep access-only and capability diagnoses
separate when the witnesses and frame are stipulated.

**Residual risk:** a real domain may make access constitutive of capability or
make the common task vocabulary theory-laden. That outcome favors the
operational-context rival or a construction-indexed diagnosis.

### 4. Source leakage

**Attack:** use GU source narratives, visible method artifacts, or mailbox
context as if they were an accepted frozen packet or receiver-side evidence.

**Controls:**

- accepted real packet count and completed real gate-run count are zero;
- GU-001 remains `NOT_YET_IMPORTABLE` and founding motivation only;
- provenance is `BLOCKED`;
- receiving assessment and full gate run are `NOT_ADMISSIBLE_NOT_RUN`;
- downstream gates are `BLOCKED_NOT_RUN`;
- GU may support neither the candidate nor a rival in this ledger; and
- receiver method count, convergence, and independence verdicts are null.

**Current finding:** pass. The synthesis references GU only to explain the
founding ownership problem and the actual provenance stop.

**Residual risk:** the charter's richer founding narrative is source context
and can be accidentally repeated as a repo-owned conclusion. Any later paper
must source every such statement through an accepted packet or clearly keep it
as owner-attributed motivation.

### 5. Artificial success

**Attack:** combine the packet contract's synthetic pass, the hierarchy's
synthetic pass, visible GU artifacts, and cross-repository narratives into one
physical success.

**Controls:**

- the joint real gate status is `NOT_RUN`;
- cross-repository partial aggregation is false;
- one argument chain and one construction branch remain required for success;
- each epistemic class has a separate role; and
- the decisive-test sequence forbids one packet or repo from completing a
  different blocked packet or gate.

**Current finding:** pass. The candidate's plausibility is argued from
conceptual fit and error controls, not from a fictional joint result.

**Residual risk:** the abundance of working machinery may create a false sense
of physical maturity. Interface completion and evidence completion must remain
visibly separate.

### 6. Construction dependence

**Attack:** select the familiar or preferred construction, omit branches that
change the verdict, or transfer a result across representations without a
theorem or test.

**Controls:**

- construction forks, skipped types, multi-level cases, incomparability, and
  hierarchy revision are required live outcomes;
- conclusion scope is the typed-partial-diagnostic construction;
- transfer without test is false;
- the ranked program includes a substantive representation and construction-
  fork challenge; and
- disagreeing branches update toward context, plurality, or hierarchy
  revision rather than being averaged.

**Current finding:** pass at structural grade. Only pure label invariance has
been tested. Substantive reformulation invariance remains open.

**Residual risk:** source owners may disagree about which transformations are
physically equivalent. The receiver cannot resolve that disagreement by
declaring a preferred branch canonical.

## Premortem: how this program most likely fails

1. **Elegant taxonomy, no real intake.** Source issuance remains too costly,
   leaving the repo with increasingly polished synthetic instruments and no
   real discrimination. Response: keep Rank 1 above schema expansion and treat
   continued blocking as evidence about interface feasibility.
2. **Normalization does the argumentative work.** Capability results track
   reviewer choices rather than physical invariants. Response: freeze rival
   frames, blind classification, and accept construction-relative outcomes.
3. **Finality becomes a residual bucket.** Every unexplained case receives a
   prestigious label. Response: require settlement and adversarial
   factorization; keep the label candidate-only and bounded.
4. **Source context leaks into synthesis.** Rich founding narratives outrun
   packet provenance. Response: use blocked records as stops, not partial
   imports, and keep source-attributed motivation visibly separate.
5. **One framework wins by vocabulary.** Source concepts are renamed until
   they fit. Response: preserve source meaning, allow hierarchy revision, and
   prefer plurality over semantic distortion.
6. **The hierarchy adds no predictive value.** A dynamics or constraint account
   reproduces every discrimination more simply. Response: update toward that
   rival; do not preserve the candidate as an unnecessary extra ontology.
